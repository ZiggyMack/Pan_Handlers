# IT-108 — LLM Meaning: Competence, Reference, Intention, Uptake, Consciousness

**Status:** OPEN (Idea Trail)
**Registry:** #108 · **Opened:** 2026-07-17 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)

- **Core separation (owed — A1):** successful LLM communication does
  not settle "understanding." Keep apart: *semantic competence*
  (context-sensitive behavior that tracks meaning), *reference* (does
  the system's language hook onto the world?), *speaker intention*
  (is there one?), *audience uptake* (humans reasonably understand
  the output), and *conscious understanding* (phenomenology).
- **Seed:** SWEEP_14's LLM branch — Ziggy's resistance to "just word
  prediction" as an exhaustive description, and the four-level
  discipline that resistance earned: training objective ≠ learned
  internal organization ≠ functional performance ≠ phenomenology.
- **The modest true conclusion (binding):** predictive training can
  produce systems whose behavior tracks rich semantic and pragmatic
  structure. That is genuinely interesting — and it does not
  establish consciousness, humanlike intention, or human-grade
  semantic grounding. *Never collapse functional competence into
  understanding.*
- **The two-sided caution:** "just prediction" underdescribes what
  exists; "it understands" overdescribes what is shown. Both moves
  are compression errors on the same ladder.
- **Kinship:** IT-078 (consciousness-first freeze rule); IT-081 (the
  explanatory-gap ladder); IT-095 (which attitude does the work — the
  attitude ledger applied to machines is this trail's far edge); the
  circle's own method (AI-mediated debate makes this trail
  self-referential — the archive runs on the systems it is asking
  about).
- **Unfinished:** what behavioral or architectural evidence would
  move any of the five layers; whether "reference" for an LLM is
  settled by use, training provenance, or deployment context.
