# IT-049 — Defeaters, Evidential Centrality, and Cumulative Belief Damage

**Status:** OPEN
**Registry:** #49 · **Opened:** 2026-07-17 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)
*(Field-desk id at ordering: "IT-050" — recurring off-by-one; registry id is canonical.)*

- **Core trajectory:** reason → rebutting or undercutting defeat →
  centrality → remaining support → confidence adjustment → suspension or
  abandonment.
- **Seed:** COMPOUND_SWEEP_08's strongest mature family — the
  Reason-Defeat / Belief-Revision Audit with the defeater taxonomy:
  **rebutting** (evidence P is false) vs **undercutting** (evidence the
  supporting route is unreliable) — most argument criticism is
  undercutting.
- **The pre-defeat centrality record (mandatory safeguard):** *before
  criticism* — how much confidence rode on R? was R publicly described as
  essential? would the belief exist without R? *After criticism* — did
  R's claimed importance suddenly change? The cleanest way to distinguish
  genuine evidential weighting from retrospective insulation.
- **The decision tree carried:** was R central? → does strong independent
  support remain? → is the replacement credible or ad hoc? → modest
  change / reduce-and-reassess / provisional repair / suspend-or-abandon
  entitlement. *Determines rational support, never proposition falsity.*
- **Cumulative accounting (the missing discipline):** related defeats must
  accumulate rather than being isolated one at a time — the immunization
  pattern's signature is per-failure quarantine.
- **Branches:** IT-045 (the revision dynamics); IT-048 (repair vs
  immunization — where the tree's leaves land); IT-050 (the asymmetry
  this trail's tools would correct).
- **Unfinished:** the tree run on a live defeated belief; how damage
  propagates among related reasons; confidence floors.
