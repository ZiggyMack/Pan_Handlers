# IT-045 — Belief, Justification, Confidence, and Revision

**Status:** OPEN
**Registry:** #45 · **Opened:** 2026-07-17 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)
*(Field-desk id at ordering: "IT-046" — recurring off-by-one; registry id is canonical.)*

- **Core trajectory:** belief → reasons → defeaters → confidence →
  suspension → abandonment.
- **Seed:** COMPOUND_SWEEP_08's central question — *when a person's stated
  reasons fail, what exactly has been defeated: the argument, the
  entitlement, the confidence, the belief, or the proposition?*
- **Unlocking distinction:** the six-status layering (truth / belief /
  propositional justification / doxastic justification / knowledge /
  action) with the propositional-doxastic gap ruled central: *good
  evidence exists ≠ this person's belief is properly based on it.*
- **The mature defeat rule:** only argument-level defeat is automatic;
  confidence revision is proportional to centrality; falsity requires
  rebutting evidence or cumulative collapse — and even then, lost
  entitlement ≠ falsity.
- **Graded confidence (binding):** 0.85 → central support weakened → 0.55
  → inquiry or suspension — never retain-unchanged-or-abandon-completely.
- **Branches:** IT-027 (the SW06 belief/warrant layers — this trail is the
  revision dynamics of that statics); IT-049 (defeaters and cumulative
  damage); IT-048 (repair vs immunization).
- **Unfinished:** whether belief is voluntary, involuntary, or normatively
  responsive to evidence (the "should adopt" question); the doxastic-gap
  test on a live case.
