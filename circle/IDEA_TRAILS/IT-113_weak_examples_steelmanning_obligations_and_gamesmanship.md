# IT-113 — Weak Examples, Steelmanning Obligations, and Gamesmanship

**Status:** OPEN (Idea Trail)
**Registry:** #113 · **Opened:** 2026-07-17 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)

- **Core instrument (formalized at SW15 Stage 3):**
  **Support-Token–Thesis Separation** — *example E supports thesis T;
  E is defeated; therefore E no longer supports T* — not
  automatically: *T is false, T lacks other support, T has been
  completely defeated.* Contested ≠ Defeated's sharper local form.
- **The three simultaneous burdens (binding):** the claimant bore the
  initial burden to present adequate support; example-defeat ≠
  thesis-defeat; and a gamesmanship charge needs separate evidence of
  *opponent knowledge + conversational obligation + total-defeat
  overclaiming*.
- **Seed:** SWEEP_15's Epstein-related dispute — the weak example
  defeated, the stronger evidence later alleged, the responsibility
  shifting toward opponents (the confirmed A3-05: the
  weak-presentation burden not sustained).
- **The game classification gate:** steelmanning obligations depend
  on the conversational game — cooperative inquiry / formal
  adversarial debate / casual argument / performance. *Failure to
  repair an opponent's case ≠ automatically gamesmanship*; the
  opponents' knowledge of stronger evidence stays UNKNOWN.
- **"Self-straw-manning" stays metaphorical** → Weak Self-Presentation
  (weak case selection ≠ self-distortion) — and *claimant's belief
  that stronger evidence exists ≠ opponent must supply it*.
- **Kinship:** OP-009 / Contested ≠ Defeated (registry); IT-090 (the
  credit problem — burdens and their owners); the Weak
  Presentation–Local Defeat–Retrospective Stronger Case–Gamesmanship
  loop (relationship ledger); IT-051's public-reason norm.
- **Unfinished:** when (if ever) an opponent's silence about known
  stronger evidence becomes culpable; a workable norm for
  representative-example selection; whether "the stronger evidence"
  ever materialized (A0 in the bounded thread).
