# IT-056 — Boredom as Awareness of Failed Engagement

**Status:** OPEN (auxiliary phenomenology — split from SWEEP_09 by field-desk order)
**Registry:** #56 · **Opened:** 2026-07-17 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)
*(Field-desk id at ordering: "IT-057" — recurring off-by-one; registry id is canonical.)*

- **Core trajectory:** awareness → low salience → temporal drag → desire
  for engagement → attentional or existential response.
- **Seed:** Ziggy's binary question (does boredom require awareness or its
  absence?) answered by Nova's **Binary-to-Gradient Conversion**: too
  little awareness → no registered boredom; awareness of disengagement →
  boredom; altered attentional depth → boredom may change.
- **Stabilized formulation:** *boredom requires enough consciousness to
  register disengagement, but its causes may include attentional
  shallowness, lack of meaning, environmental monotony, low agency,
  fatigue, or mood.*
- **⚠️ The awareness-equivocation risk (this trail's central caution):**
  minimal consciousness / reflective awareness / attentional depth /
  contemplative richness are all called "awareness" — *the gradient may
  solve the paradox by changing meanings between levels*; one variable
  must be held stable.
- **Deficiency Reattribution HEAVILY QUARANTINED:** "the world is boring →
  perhaps attention is impoverished" is one hypothesis — never a general
  account; environmental countercheck required (coercive repetition,
  deprivation, depression, exhaustion, low autonomy); **no subject-blame
  account of boredom.**
- **Branches:** IT-055 (the attention model boredom stresses); five forms
  noted at Stage 1 (situational / attentional / existential / agitation /
  apathetic underarousal).
- **Unfinished:** whether deep awareness can *intensify* rather than
  dissolve boredom (registered, untested); boredom vs apathy vs
  restlessness vs depression boundaries; any empirical contact.
