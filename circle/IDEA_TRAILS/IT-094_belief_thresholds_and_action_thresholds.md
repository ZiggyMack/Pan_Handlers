# IT-094 — Belief Thresholds and Action Thresholds

**Status:** OPEN (Idea Trail)
**Registry:** #94 · **Opened:** 2026-07-17 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)
*(Field-desk id at ordering: "IT-097" — numbering drift; registry id is canonical.)*

- **Core distinction:** *What should I believe?* and *What should I
  do?* answer to different thresholds. Belief tracks evidential
  support; action is set by decision rules that weigh values, costs,
  options, and stakes on top of credence.
- **Seed:** SWEEP_13's strongest surviving challenge — evidence and
  credence do not by themselves determine action — and its most
  consequential early omission (confirmed A3-1: the "atheists have
  faith" discussion ran long before the separation was performed).
- **The bleed (binding):** without the separation, *uncertain action*
  and *insufficiently evidenced belief* merge — ordinary justified
  action under uncertainty starts to look like belief beyond evidence.
  Sibling repair (confirmed A3-2): **incomplete evidence ≠ inadequate
  evidence** — insufficient for certainty, for justified belief, and
  for a particular action are three different insufficiencies.
- **Settled by the dig:** identity NOT established; complete
  independence NOT established; **linked-but-distinct strongly
  supported**.
- **Kinship:** IT-093 (trivialization); IT-100 (the completion
  question); IT-095 (the attitude ledger).
- **Unfinished:** an explicit credence threshold for belief (A1 —
  Grant's was never given); whether a belief-rule and an action-rule
  can ever be shown identical for any agent.
