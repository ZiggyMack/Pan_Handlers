# IT-119 — The Desire Taxonomy and Motivational Dominance

**Status:** OPEN (Idea Trail)
**Registry:** #119 · **Opened:** 2026-07-17 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)

- **Core problem:** "you always do what you want" is near-tautological
  under the winning-motivation reading — every intentional act
  expresses the strongest operative motivation *by definition*, which
  makes the claim uninformative. Nine senses of "want" separated
  (immediate desire; strongest motivating preference;
  all-things-considered preference; endorsed desire; intention;
  chosen action; desired outcome; uncoerced choice; higher-order
  identification).
- **The work example (binding):** a person who goes to work may
  simultaneously not want to work, want income, want to avoid firing,
  resent the options, choose the least bad one, and wish the choice
  architecture were different. *Action reveals motivational dominance
  ≠ action satisfies the agent's desires; least-bad choice ≠
  unqualified wanting; internal preference cause ≠ absence of
  coercive structure.*
- **The agency refinement:** "agency within vs over the system" →
  **first-order agency** (choosing among current options) vs
  **structural agency** (acting to revise options, rules, incentives)
  — with the metaphysical guard: structural agency is still action
  *within* a larger causal system; no supernatural-escape reading.
- **The five live classifications:** revealed-preference analysis /
  motivational description / compatibilist action theory / semantic
  redefinition / near-tautology — the bounded evidence does not
  choose among them.
