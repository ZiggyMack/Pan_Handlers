# IT-125 — The Starting Whole and the Authority of Origin

**Status:** OPEN (Idea Trail)
**Registry:** #125 · **Opened:** 2026-07-20 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)

- **Core five-way (binding):** causal-origin (broadly compatible with
  revision) / constitutive-dependence (also compatible) /
  **epistemic-closure** (much stronger — NOT established) /
  practical-constraint (silent on unselected accessible alternatives)
  / **normative-authority** (needs its own argument — *causal origin
  never automatically confers legitimacy*). The central ambiguity:
  *all transformation has antecedent conditions* ≠ *no transformation
  can alter the authority or organization of those conditions.*
- **The unconscious argument, corrected (the packet's sharpest
  repair):** Ziggy's Jungian objection is **epistemic, not
  ontological** — it defeats claims of transparent self-mastery
  (complete self-survey; consciously known limits) but does NOT
  prove choices transcend the whole or that reflective agency
  creates values ex nihilo. The **Whole-Mastery Interpretation**
  (Grant must believe he consciously controls/maps the whole) stays
  **QUARANTINED** as a hidden-premise reconstruction across all four
  stages — Grant may need only causal or constitutive continuity.
- **The four-way firewall (binding):** origin ≠ psychological
  ownership ≠ practical control ≠ normative justification (*inherited
  causes ≠ endorsed ≠ justified; arose from the whole ≠ unrevisable;
  revision also has causes ≠ revision is unreal*). Ziggy's sufficient
  modest thesis: reflective processes can reorganize the authority,
  salience, and expression of inherited values even if those
  processes are themselves caused — compatibilism-friendly, no
  creation from nothing required (Origin–Endorsement–Revision–
  Justification Separation, a cautious profile candidate).
- **Kinship:** IT-124 (the lens ladder — the practical-constraint
  reading is its twin); AUX_07's reciprocal bedrock rule (the
  regress-relocated-not-eliminated finding); SW11's consciousness
  layer (hard-problem incompleteness ≠ which completion is correct —
  the same shape one level up).
- **Unfinished:** the Conditioned-Agency Test (A1 — can inhibition,
