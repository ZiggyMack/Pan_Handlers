# IT-129 — The Higher-Reason Regress and the Victory-Condition Question

**Status:** OPEN (Idea Trail)
**Registry:** #129 · **Opened:** 2026-07-21 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)

- **Core finding — Grant's regress challenge (G-QUOTED, packet-local
  only):** choose a reason for action you believe can change; if no
  higher reason exists to change it, Grant declares victory because
  it will never change; if a higher reason exists, the game restarts
  at that reason; the stated terminal condition is only Grant's
  victory. Repaired across all four stages from an initial reading as
  a decisive "infinite regress trap" into a narrower, more defensible
  question: does rational revision terminate in a basic commitment or
  regress indefinitely, and what would count as a legitimate
  foundation?
- **The load-bearing repair:** *"no higher reason identified" does
  NOT entail "the reason can never change."* A reason can change
  through new first-order evidence, discovered inconsistency,
  same-level conflicting considerations, reflective equilibrium, or
  causal/psychological change — none of which need be hierarchically
  "higher." "Higher reason" is itself multiply ambiguous (more
  general / logically prior / normatively stronger / motivationally
  dominant / meta-level / justificatorily foundational). **Foundation
  does not imply immutability** — a terminal reason can still be
  defeasible, revisable, or reinterpreted.
- **Nova's reciprocal move, scoped:** applying Grant's own rule to
  itself (what higher reason justifies the rule that reasons need
  higher reasons?) pressures the game's consistency but does **not**
  by itself refute it — filed as **Reciprocal Rule and Victory-
  Condition Audit**, a Nova-generated evaluative protocol, only a
  partial role-induced implementation of Symmetry Testing of
  Standards. If Grant's intended conclusion is merely that every
  hierarchy terminates or continues, self-application may *confirm*
  rather than defeat his thesis. The positive task — a coherent model
  of revision, foundation, or reflective equilibrium — remains
  undone on both sides.
- **What "I win" might mean (unresolved, A0 evidence gap):** a
  foundation exists; the stated reason is immutable; revision lacks
  justification; the regress continues; rhetorical/conversational
  victory only. Whether the numbered challenge was quoted verbatim,
  reconstructed from memory, or compressed by Ziggy is itself UNKNOWN.
