# IT-106 — Explanatory Openness After Mechanism

**Status:** OPEN (Idea Trail)
**Registry:** #106 · **Opened:** 2026-07-17 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)

- **Core discipline (the two-sided rule, binding):** *a proximate
  mechanism does not deductively settle ultimate ontology* — AND —
  *logical non-exclusion does not provide positive evidence for
  transcendence*. Scientific mechanism **constrains** source
  hypotheses even where it does not logically disprove every
  metaphysical account.
- **Seed:** SWEEP_14's Mechanism–Source Non-Collapse move (one of the
  six profile-ready candidates, queue-filed) and the Implication
  Reopening pattern: mechanism conceded → "which implications
  remain?" asked.
- **The symmetric dangers:** on one side, *descriptive reductionism*
  (mechanism mistaken for significance — the perceived
  significance-compression worry); on the other, *permanent
  insulation* (every mechanism accepted while the preferred
  possibility never risks loss — the protection pattern). The
  Expansive Claim–Mechanism Translation–Implication Reopening loop is
  this trail's home dynamic, with all five readings of the middle
  step live.
- **Binding restrictions:** no
  mechanism-underdetermination-to-supernatural-support inference; no
  naturalization-to-intentional-containment inference; Grant's motive
  UNKNOWN; the openness itself owes reachable loss conditions before
  it counts as anything more than openness.
- **Kinship:** IT-104 (source models); IT-100 (the bridge audit —
  SW13's twin discipline); IT-093/IT-096 (the possibility ladders).
- **Unfinished:** the audit that says *which* implications a conceded
  mechanism actually licenses, merely permits, or leaves untouched —
  the Implication-Propagation question in its general form.
