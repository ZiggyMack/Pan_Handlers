# IT-104 — The Unconscious as Generator, Receiver, or Interface

**Status:** OPEN (Idea Trail)
**Registry:** #104 · **Opened:** 2026-07-17 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)

- **Core question:** when insight surfaces without explicit conscious
  reasoning, is the unconscious *generating* the content (internal
  cognition), *receiving* it (from an external or transpersonal
  source), or acting as an *interface* between the two? The three
  models predict the same experience — which is the problem.
- **Seed:** SWEEP_14's mechanism/source branch — unconscious cognition
  as the middle term Grant's dichotomy repair opened (⭐ G-DIRECT:
  conscious linear deduction and supernatural revelation are not the
  only options).
- **The discriminating-evidence debt (A1, binding):** no evidence was
  produced that distinguishes the generator, receiver, and interface
  models — and **the interface hypothesis owes its own failure
  condition** (*what would make it LESS credible?*). Without one,
  every naturalistic mechanism is accepted while the external-source
  possibility stays permanently insulated (the protection pattern the
  dig's own originating family exists to catch).
- **The four-level audit (binding):** proximate mechanism ≠
  developmental source ≠ informational source ≠ ontological ground;
  *mechanism does not settle ontology* AND *non-exclusion is not
  positive support* (confirmed A3-09 — later partially repaired
  in-thread).
- **Kinship:** IT-105 (altered states as the vivid case); IT-103
  (interface in theological dress); IT-106 (the openness-after-
  mechanism discipline).
- **Unfinished:** any concrete discriminator (predictive novelty?
  information the person could not have had? convergence across
  isolated subjects?) — and who bears its burden.
