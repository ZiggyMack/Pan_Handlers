- **Core trajectory:** predictive success → empirical adequacy → truth →
  utility → ultimate authority.
- **Seed:** DIG_AUX_07's prediction branch — the pilot's "prediction as
  ultimate standard" theme in its most direct bounded form yet (still
  reported-only).
- **The G1–G6 instrument (this trail's spine):** empirical necessity /
  comparative criterion / public adjudication / naturalized cognition /
  predictive architecture / **predictive sovereignty** — only G6 is the
  "epistemic monarch" thesis the Zero-Pipeline hypothesis would need;
  the bounded thread cannot place Grant on the ladder.
- **Unlocking distinction:** prediction ≠ truth (five relations: evidence
  of truth / adequacy / usefulness / calibration / intervention); a model
  may predict well while idealizing, misdescribing ontology, or sharing
  predictions with rivals. The four-level promotion ladder binds:
  *participation ≠ centrality ≠ priority ≠ sovereignty.*
- **The necessary/sufficient thesis (the cleanest charitable form, never
  demonstrated in-thread):** predictive adequacy may be *necessary* for
  publicly asserted empirical claims without being *sufficient* for truth,
  explanation, meaning, or universal epistemic authority.
- **Broad-definition triviality guard:** if "prediction" includes all
  inference or cognition, "prediction is ultimate" becomes vacuous —
  definitions must be fixed before the ordering dispute means anything.
