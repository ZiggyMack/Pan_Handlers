# IT-114 — Counterpossibles and Divine Identity

**Status:** OPEN (Idea Trail)
**Registry:** #114 · **Opened:** 2026-07-17 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)

- **Core four-way (binding):** an atrocious divine command may be an
  *ordinary counterfactual* (God could but doesn't), an
  *epistemic-shock scenario* (something apparently divine commands
  what the believer holds evil), a **counterpossible** (a necessarily
  good God commands cruelty — a metaphysically impossible
  antecedent), or an *identity-revision case* (the commander would no
  longer satisfy the target conception of God).
- **Seed:** SWEEP_16's divine-command formalization dispute — Grant's
  fixed conditional is valid under his stipulation; but validity does
  not determine how a counterpossible is evaluated, whether it
  reveals a dependency relation, whether the conclusion is vacuous or
  theory-revising, or whether "God" keeps the same referent across
  the hypothetical.
- **The discipline (binding):** *improbable antecedent ≠ impossible
  antecedent*; *impossible antecedent ≠ meaningless hypothetical* —
  counterpossibles can still do philosophical work (exposing what
  depends on what), they just do different work than ordinary
  conditionals.
- **The open question:** Ziggy's reformulation ("if your God acted in
  a way you were certain God would never act…") captures the
  epistemic conflict — does it illuminate Grant's original, or
  replace it with a different thought experiment? (The
  alternative-hypothetical fidelity test, A1.)
- **Kinship:** IT-096 (the modal ladder); IT-103 (which God-concept
  fixes the identity conditions); IT-100 (bridge audits); Family 2's
  stipulation-vs-attribution triple.
- **Unfinished:** a working semantics for evaluating counterpossibles
  in theological debate; who bears the burden of showing the
  antecedent possible or impossible.
