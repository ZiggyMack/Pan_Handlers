# IT-121 — The Interpretive Target Ledger

**Status:** OPEN (Idea Trail)
**Registry:** #121 · **Opened:** 2026-07-18 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)

- **Core instrument (SW18's lasting yield):** six objects held apart —
  what the sentence says / what the speaker intended / what follows
  under interpretation R / what may fairly be attributed as belief /
  whether the wording generated reasonable confusion / **whether the
  reporter represented the critic as testing an implication or
  personally endorsing it**. *No result from one ledger silently
  settles another.*
- **The firewall (binding):** *"the sentence implies X" ≠ "the
  speaker consciously believes X"; "the speaker did not intend X" ≠
  "X cannot follow under another reading"; "the critic tests X" ≠
  "the critic personally believes X".* The five-claim ladder fixes
  which claim a critic actually made before either side is scored.
- **The core failure dynamic:** the Implication–Attribution Drift
  Loop — *consequence identified → heard as attribution → consequence
  /belief distinguished → shift to functional misrepresentation →
  access-to-intent challenged → **the original target disappears*** —
  with the global repair: **movement to another interpretive target
  requires explicit relabeling.**
- **The middle position filed:** communicative responsibility — a
  speaker may not believe Q yet bear responsibility if the wording
  predictably suggests Q (*belief responsibility ≠ communication
  responsibility* — the critic can be right about the second while
  wrong about the first).
- **Kinship:** IT-115 (compressed models — the reporter's side of the
  same territory); the Method-Trade-Off Audit (wording stress-testing
  vs intent recovery — fix the success condition first); Grice/
  Austin–Searle/Davidson noted by the field desk as the traveled
  road.
- **Unfinished:** the Relative-Warrant Audit (both interpretations
  infer ≠ equally warranted — A1); the speaker self-report
  jurisdiction map; the debate correction-and-exit protocol.
