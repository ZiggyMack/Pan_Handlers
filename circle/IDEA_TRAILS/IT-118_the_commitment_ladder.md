# IT-118 — The Commitment Ladder

**Status:** OPEN (Idea Trail)
**Registry:** #118 · **Opened:** 2026-07-17 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)

- **Core instrument (SW17's centerpiece):** the nine-rung ladder —
  hypothesis generation → comparative credence → provisional ranking
  → inquiry prioritization → working-model selection → practical use
  → decision-rule adoption → normative endorsement →
  exclusive/foundational commitment. **A person may occupy one rung
  without occupying the next, and every upward transition owes
  evidence.**
- **Seed:** the "did Grant decide?" dispute — six incompatible acts
  hiding inside one word. The finished prey: *a comparative credence
  is not automatically a voluntarily chosen belief, practical
  adoption, or moral commitment* — and the strongest operation is
  making each transition visible, never proving that credence is
  secretly a decision.
- **The twin disciplines (binding):** the horse-race split (*which
  horse seems likely* ≠ *which horse receives the bet* — Grant may be
  right about the first and Ziggy about the second) and the
  jurisdiction rule (the corporate identify-assess-gather-decide
  protocol governs action, never the involuntary formation of
  credences — *unresolved knowledge gap ≠ no rational provisional
  ranking*).
- **The agency face:** doxastic ≠ inquiry ≠ practical agency
  (compatibilism ≠ doxastic voluntarism; a contradiction requires the
  SAME agency relation affirmed and denied); the offloading diagnosis
  quarantined with its self-sealing guard (*without a reachable
  defeater, resistance confirms the diagnosis by definition*).
- **Kinship:** IT-094 (belief/action thresholds — this ladder is its
  generalization); IT-095 (the attitude ledger); IT-097 (choosing
  belief vs practice); IT-067's discriminator (the disconfirmation
  test).
- **Unfinished:** where Grant's reported suffering claim actually
  sits on the ladder (A0); the reverse-role test of the decision
  standard (A1); rung-transition evidence standards in general.
