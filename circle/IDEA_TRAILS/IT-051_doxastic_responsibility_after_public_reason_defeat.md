# IT-051 — Doxastic Responsibility after Public Reason Defeat

**Status:** OPEN
**Registry:** #51 · **Opened:** 2026-07-17 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)
*(Field-desk id at ordering: "IT-052" — recurring off-by-one; registry id is canonical.)*

- **Core trajectory:** reason publicly offered → reason defeated → support
  claim withdrawn → remaining warrant disclosed → confidence recalibrated
  → rational entitlement reassessed.
- **Seed:** COMPOUND_SWEEP_08's corrective to its own defensive emphasis
  (confirmed A3): *even when an offered reason did not cause the belief,
  once publicly presented as support it becomes fair game — and its
  defeat obliges the believer.*
- **The mature norm (the trail's instrument):** critic defeats R → critic
  has defeated *R as offered public support* (not P); the believer now
  owes — withdrawal of R; recalibration of claimed warrant; disclosure of
  remaining support; reduced confidence if R was central. *The critic
  need not prove the belief false before the believer has any
  responsibility to update.*
- **The balancing clause:** this norm answers the Genesis–Warrant escape
  risk (IT-046) — "that wasn't my real reason" cannot cancel the debts
  incurred by offering it publicly; but neither does public defeat reach
  private entitlement automatically (the burden ladder from IT-031
  applies: what is owed scales with the authority that was claimed).
- **Branches:** IT-046 (the escape route this norm closes); IT-048 (the
  repair procedure's first step — explicit withdrawal); IT-027/IT-031
  (public-authority scaling); DEBATES/ (the future home — this norm is a
  debate-conduct rule as much as an epistemic one).
- **Unfinished:** the norm applied to a live circle debate; what
  withdrawal looks like in practice; whether the circle adopts it as a
  procedural standard (candidate charter-norm material).
