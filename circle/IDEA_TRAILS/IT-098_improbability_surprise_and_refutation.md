# IT-098 — Improbability, Surprise, and Refutation

**Status:** OPEN (Idea Trail)
**Registry:** #98 · **Opened:** 2026-07-17 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)
*(Field-desk id at ordering: "IT-101" — numbering drift; registry id is canonical.)*

- **Core separation (demonstrated in-packet):** ex ante expectation ≠
  ex post acceptance. Prior probability rationally guides expectation
  *before* the outcome; once an outcome is established, its prior
  improbability does not refute it. **Surprise is psychology, not
  counter-evidence; improbability is not impossibility.**
- **Seed:** SWEEP_13's raffle stress test — the winning-ticket case
  Ziggy used against Grant's likelihood rule, and the psychological
  conversion of low probability into rejection.
- **The A1 extension (never asserted as demonstrated):** the raffle
  never separated evidence *sources* — direct observation vs official
  record vs testimony vs dubious report vs fraud vs measurement or
  procedural error — or how source quality moves posterior belief.
  The analogy remains only partially disciplined; what it was aimed
  at (impossible-vs-improbable? testimony? divine low-probability
  claims?) stays open.
- **Binding restriction:** no low-probability-to-impossibility
  attribution to Grant — the accusation is not built into any label.
- **Kinship:** IT-096 (possibility ladder); IT-081 (the three-gap
  ladder — *not currently explained ≠ impossible* is this trail's
  modal cousin).
- **Unfinished:** the Bayesian formalization (likelihood ratios for
  testimony vs observation); when a surprising report *should*
  lower confidence in the reporter rather than the event.
