# IT-059 — Rational Permission, Recommendation, and Requirement

**Status:** OPEN
**Registry:** #59 · **Opened:** 2026-07-17 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)
*(Field-desk id at ordering: "IT-060" — recurring off-by-one; registry id is canonical.)*

- **Core trajectory:** possible → permissible to consider → reasonable to
  investigate → recommended to believe → required to believe → irrational
  to reject.
- **Seed:** SWEEP_09's normative ladder — the rungs the
  plausibility-to-irrationality bridge silently skips; *no
  rational-permission → rational-requirement slide* is the trail's
  founding restriction.
- **Unlocking distinction:** a comparative ranking may create *some*
  rational pressure without strict obligation — the force depends on the
  evidential gap, suspension availability, belief-vs-action, stakes,
  contestable priors, and whether multiple doxastic attitudes stay
  permissible.
- **Kinship (cross-excavation):** the belief-side of IT-050's abandonment
  problem (when must confidence move?) and the mirror of SW08's
  preservation bias — this trail asks when retention stops being
  *permissible*; IT-050 asks when preservation must *end*. Together they
  are the two halves of the missing normative rulebook.
- **The voluntariness question carried:** whether belief is voluntary,
  involuntary, or normatively responsive determines what "required to
  believe" can even mean (SW08's unresolved question, resurfacing).
- **Branches:** IT-052 (the promotion this ladder disciplines); IT-045
  (graded confidence); IT-050 (the abandonment face).
- **Unfinished:** the threshold rules for each rung; whether "irrational
  to reject" is ever established short of overwhelming evidence; a
  G-DIRECT instance of anyone actually claiming the top rung.
