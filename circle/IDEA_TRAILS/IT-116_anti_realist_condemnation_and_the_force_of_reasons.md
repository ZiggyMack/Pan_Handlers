# IT-116 — Anti-Realist Condemnation and the Force of Reasons

**Status:** OPEN (Idea Trail — ⭐ first Tom/Tapioca-seeded trail)
**Registry:** #116 · **Opened:** 2026-07-17 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)

- **Core finding (binding):** anti-realism and moral condemnation are
  **not formally contradictory** — one may coherently hold that
  morality is not a mind-independent object while condemning beliefs
  under endorsed or justifiable norms (Tapioca's direct
  counterposition, carried as active counterevidence). The real
  question is the condemnation's **authority, scope, and binding
  force** — not its consistency.
- **The force ladder (completion architecture):** causal motivation →
  desire-based/prudential reasons → agent-relative reasons → moral
  reasons → intersubjective justification → social enforcement →
  categorical obligation → objective moral fact. *Suffering motivates
  ≠ suffering obligates; harm gives some reasons ≠ harm gives
  categorical overriding reasons.*
- **Seeds:** Tom's Harm-as-Reason Grounding (T-DIRECT-SCREENSHOT —
  harm, suffering, human reality, and social function as
  reason-givers; an *informal but real* grounding attempt, exact
  metaethic unresolved among seven readings) and his "by definition"
  move (*definition ≠ justification; primitive axiom ≠ circularity; a
  disputed definition cannot settle the dispute by being labeled
  "definition"* — with the scope counterexamples owed: surgery,
  defense, punishment, sacrifice, tragic choice).
- **The reciprocal restriction (binding):** never assume only
  mind-independent categorical authority counts as legitimate
  normativity — constructivist and intersubjective alternatives are
  live; Ziggy's realist demand is itself a commitment owing grounds
  (the reciprocal-grounding A1).
- **Kinship:** IT-101 (the terminus question); IT-095 (the attitude
  ledger); Family 6/7 architecture; Tom's compatibilist agency
  position (the same under-specification pattern).
