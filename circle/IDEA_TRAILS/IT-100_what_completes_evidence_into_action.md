# IT-100 — What Completes Evidence into Action?

**Status:** OPEN (Idea Trail)
**Registry:** #100 · **Opened:** 2026-07-17 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)
*(Field-desk id at ordering: "IT-103" — numbering drift; registry id is canonical.)*

- **Core question:** evidence and credence underdetermine action —
  something completes the transition. Is the completing machinery
  *faith*, or just ordinary practical reason?
- **The decision-theoretic default (confirmed A3-6):** `credence +
  values + costs + options + decision rule → action`. Before naming
  any extra ingredient "faith," test whether this ordinary bridge
  already explains the transition — then ask what *additional
  explanatory work* the word performs (faith's explanatory
  remainder).
- **Seed:** SWEEP_13's Practical–Ontological Bridge Audit — the
  five-arrow chain (*uncertain action → decision rule/commitment →
  "faith" → religious commitment → God*), every arrow needing its own
  argument. Notably: the Stage-2 extractor omitted this family
  entirely and the field desk restored it; its earlier absence in the
  conversation is confirmed A3-5.
- **Binding restrictions:** no residual-uncertainty-to-faith
  inference by definition; no minimal-faith-to-religious-faith
  inference; no practical-underdetermination-to-God inference. A
  practical gap never carries ontological freight by itself.
- **Kinship:** IT-094 (thresholds); IT-093 (trivialization); IT-101
  (where the regress of grounds terminates); IT-095 (which attitude
  does the work).
- **Unfinished:** action without propositional belief (A1 — habit,
  reflex, embodied skill, automatic response); whether any residue
  survives once values, costs, and rules are fully accounted.
