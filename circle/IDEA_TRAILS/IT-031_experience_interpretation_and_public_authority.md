- **Core trajectory:** first-person event → testimony → psychological
  transformation → causal interpretation → metaphysical claim → public or
  coercive authority.
- **Seed:** COMPOUND_SWEEP_06's deepest internal correction — **Ziggy's own
  teapot framework is the best tool for disciplining spirituality**: *A
  experiences divine presence; the experience is real as experience; the
  divine interpretation may still be false.* Preserves (never reduces) the
  five-way split: event / report / effect / interpretation / ontology.
- **Unlocking distinction:** *I experienced X ≠ my explanation of X is
  correct* — testimony can establish occurrence, phenomenology, and effects;
  it does not automatically establish external cause, ontology, universal
  obligation, or public authority.
- **The symmetry rule (both directions):** measurement is not the only
  possible evidence AND testimony is not self-authenticating ontology —
  first-person methods may be exactly right for "what did it feel like"
  while historical, causal, and metaphysical inferences remain publicly
  auditable.
- **Burden ladder carried from IT-027:** private belief < personal knowledge
  claim < public assent < institutional policy < coercive obligation — the
  authority *requested* determines the evidence *owed*.
- **Registered rejection (polarity discipline):** admitting testimony as
  evidence ≠ Generative Admission Restriction (admission ≠ weight); the
  separation is not anti-spiritual — it preserves experiential evidence
  while narrowing the inference.
