# IT-097 — Choosing Belief versus Choosing Practice

**Status:** OPEN (Idea Trail)
**Registry:** #97 · **Opened:** 2026-07-17 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)
*(Field-desk id at ordering: "IT-100" — numbering drift; registry id is canonical.)*

- **Core disambiguation (confirmed A3-7):** "choose God" splits at
  least seven ways — choose to *believe* God exists; to *trust* God;
  to adopt religious *practice*; to *investigate*; to *hope*; to
  *behave as if*; to take on a God-centered practical *identity*.
  These differ in voluntariness, evidential warrant, and practical
  content.
- **Seed:** SWEEP_13 — the "choose God" language in the
  faith-as-uncertain-action argument, where the ability to select a
  practice was sliding toward the ability to select a belief.
- **The hard rung (binding):** whether categorical belief is directly
  voluntary is `UNKNOWN` (A0) — never assume doxastic voluntarism.
  The ability to choose practice, investigation, or hope does NOT
  establish the ability to choose belief.
- **Unlocking distinction:** acting-as-if is a genuine, choosable
  commitment that requires no belief threshold — which is exactly why
  it cannot be converted into evidence that belief was chosen.
- **Kinship:** IT-096 (live options); IT-095 (the attitude ledger);
  IT-094 (thresholds — practice answers to the action threshold,
  belief to the evidential one).
- **Unfinished:** which of the seven choices Pascal-style arguments
  actually license; whether sustained practice causally shifts belief
  (an empirical question the bounded thread never reached).
