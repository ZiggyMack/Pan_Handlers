# IT-128 — Terminal Ranking, Pursuit, and Decision-Procedure Separation

**Status:** OPEN (Idea Trail)
**Registry:** #128 · **Opened:** 2026-07-21 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)

- **Core finding — SWEEP_21's central repair:** Ziggy's death-cult
  extinction counterexample to suffering minimization is not one
  argument but four separable ones — **terminal world ranking**
  (does a painless empty world outrank every inhabited world
  containing suffering?), **pursuit authorization** (does believing
  that ranking license coercive pursuit of extinction?), **decision
  procedure** (would a realistic suffering-minimizing agent actually
  initiate the campaign after weighing success probability, transition
  harm, and alternatives?), and **institutional implementation** (what
  happens when the principle is interpreted by ideological actors with
  concentrated power?). Without this separation, Ziggy's counterexample
  and Grant's reported expected-suffering reply appear to contradict
  each other while addressing different questions.
- **The load-bearing asymmetry:** Grant's strongest reported reply
  (a comparative expected-suffering calculation — probability of
  success, transition harms, uncertainty, resistance, alternatives)
  may fully answer the *pursuit* branch. It does **not** touch the
  *terminal-ranking* branch — practical infeasibility is not an
  answer to population ethics. This is the trail's central open
  question: does any suffering-only axiology, formally stated, avoid
  ranking a painless empty world above inhabited life, or does the
  pursuit-branch answer merely paper over an unresolved terminal
  commitment?
- **Kin move — Normative-Layer Separation (co-constructed, never a
  Ziggy operator):** axiology / criterion of rightness / decision
  procedure / applied ethics / institutional implementation / moral
  psychology / metaethics, seven layers. "Principle without a host,"
  "law without a judge," and "orphaned meta-ethic" all stay metaphors
  inside this architecture — none establish that a valid moral
  principle must be agentive, or that implementation-dependence shows
  the axiology false.
- **The derivation test (packet-level, not yet resolved):** does
  catastrophe follow from the criterion plus reasonable empirical
  beliefs (an internal problem), or only after adding false
  probabilities, illicit aggregation, or hidden ideological premises
  (misuse)? Neither "bad actors did it" nor "the metric licensed it"
  is sufficient without showing the derivation.
- **Kinship:** IT-017 (evil, world selection, and moral generativity —
  the same shape of unbridged inference, different domain: a
  well-known logical distinction with a missing bridge to the scalar
  conclusion); IT-100 (what completes evidence into action); IT-101
  (God/brute-fact/ignorance — SWEEP_20's category-error repair,
  same dig-family); the Catastrophic-Permission/Internal-Adequacy
  Challenge (the renamed problem-of-evil analogy — Ziggy's own
  disanalogy catch: God is agentive/choosing/responsible, a metric is
  an abstract standard evaluated for adequacy, and agentive
  responsibility does not transfer automatically).
- **Unfinished:** population-ethics commitments for either party

  average vs. distribution-sensitive aggregation); whether any
  positive value beyond suffering reduction is required to block the
  terminal ranking; Ziggy's own moral alternative has not yet met the
  same completeness burden he demands of Grant's criterion (the
  reciprocal accounting question, IT-077-adjacent).
