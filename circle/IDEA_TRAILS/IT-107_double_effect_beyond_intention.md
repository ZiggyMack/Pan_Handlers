# IT-107 — Double Effect Beyond Intention

**Status:** OPEN (Idea Trail)
**Registry:** #107 · **Opened:** 2026-07-17 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)

- **Core caution (binding):** the principle of double effect has more
  conditions than intent alone — act type, means/end structure, and
  proportionality all bear weight. The thread's simplified
  intent-centered examples (including the abortion applications) must
  not stand in for the full doctrine.
- **Seed:** SWEEP_14's Intention-Hinge Testing — a consequential
  *one-time* move (never an operator): the same physical act's moral
  classification made to hinge on intention.
- **What the one-time move legitimately showed:** intention is a real
  hinge in moral classification — the difference between foreseen and
  intended harm does work in ordinary and doctrinal ethics alike.
- **What it did not show:** that intent is the *only* hinge; that the
  doctrine's other conditions were satisfied or even consulted; how
  the doctrine applies to any concrete unspecified medical case (A0 —
  exact judgments unavailable in the bounded source).
- **Kinship:** IT-094 (thresholds — classification hinges generally);
  Family 4 (Meaning–Intent and Interpretation — intention as a hinge
  in *interpretive* classification is the same shape one level up);
  Family 8 (moral authority).
- **Unfinished:** the full four-condition analysis run on even one
  concrete case; whether Ziggy's intention-centering is a stable view
  or a local argumentative move.
