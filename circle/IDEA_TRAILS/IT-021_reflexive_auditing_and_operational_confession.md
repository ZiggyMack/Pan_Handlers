> **Central research question (registered 2026-07-17):**
> *When a framework names its master and confesses its price, what procedure
> determines whether that price is acceptable rather than merely honestly
> disclosed?* — **disclosure exposes the throne; it does not legitimate the
> ruler.**

- **Core trajectory:** framework comparison → MASTER / PRICE / DISTORTIONS /
  FRUITS → audit the criteria → price the evaluator → CFA self-application →
  regress and tool-temple risk → proximate confession → return to action.
- **Primary candidate:** **Reflexive Audit with Operational Closure** — a
  shared protocol (attribution preserved: *Claude formulates → Ziggy and Nova
  adopt → CFA architecture revised*); HIGH confidence as protocol, lower as
  any individual's personality operator. The stopping rule is part of the
  operator — audit recursion without closure is paralysis.
- **Unlocking distinctions:** confession ≠ justification (disclosed ≠
  coherent ≠ evidentially supported ≠ morally acceptable); transparency
  sufficient for action ≠ ultimate justification; the disclosure block is a
  *protocol*, not a neutral scoring algorithm — "fruits" and "distortions"
  are themselves evaluated under some master.
- **Standing risk (registered):** *disclosure without revision may become
  ritualized immunity* — the procedure needs consequences for unacceptable
  prices, or confession becomes a liturgy.
- **Tool-Temple maxim** (governance ledger): a method should not displace the
  purpose it was designed to serve — the danger's recognition is evidenced
  more strongly than the inhibition's successful use.
