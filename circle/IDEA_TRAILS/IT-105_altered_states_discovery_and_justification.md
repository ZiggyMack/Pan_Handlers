# IT-105 — Altered States, Discovery, and Justification

**Status:** OPEN (Idea Trail)
**Registry:** #105 · **Opened:** 2026-07-17 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)

- **Core separation (binding):** *discovery process ≠ justification;
  unusual route to a hypothesis ≠ evidence the hypothesis came from
  outside cognition; successful result ≠ supernatural causal proof.*
- **Seed:** SWEEP_14's ayahuasca branch — the combinatorial-
  improbability argument ("statistical miracle" of finding the right
  plant combination) pressed toward a supernatural information source.
- **The foundation audit that was owed (confirmed A3-04):** the
  improbability claim was never tested through an actual search model
  — number of trials, cumulative cultural experimentation across
  generations, intermediate observable effects (taste, toxicity),
  gradual recipe modification, survivorship bias, independent
  discovery paths, *unknown probability treated as extraordinary
  improbability*.
- **The strongest recoverable claim:** altered states may have
  influenced hypothesis generation, attention, ritual experimentation,
  or interpretation of plant effects — which is real and interesting,
  and distinct from *altered states supplied accurate medicinal
  information from outside cognition*.
- **⭐ Grant's contribution (G-DIRECT):** the false-dichotomy repair —
  conscious linear deduction and supernatural revelation are not the
  only options; unconscious cognition, embodied learning, social
  transmission, and cumulative experimentation occupy the middle. The
  field desk ruled the objection *potentially legitimate*.
- **Kinship:** IT-104 (the three source models); IT-098 (improbability
  discipline — surprise is psychology); IT-100 (what completes
  evidence).
- **Unfinished:** the actual ethnobotanical search history (an
  empirical question no bounded thread can settle); whether any
  discovery-context fact could ever carry justificatory weight.
