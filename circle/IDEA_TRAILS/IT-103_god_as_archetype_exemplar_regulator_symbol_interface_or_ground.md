# IT-103 — God as Archetype: Exemplar, Regulator, Symbol, Interface, or Ground

**Status:** OPEN (Idea Trail)
**Registry:** #103 · **Opened:** 2026-07-17 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)

- **Core disambiguation (owed — A1):** "God as moral archetype" splits
  at least five ways — moral *exemplar*; *psychic regulator* (highest-
  order organizing symbol in the person); *cultural symbol* (shared
  narratives, ideals, sanctions); *interface* (a real channel to
  something beyond the psyche); *metaphysical ground* (the actual
  source of goodness).
- **The level wall (binding):** the psychological and cultural levels
  do not prove the metaphysical level — and the metaphysical claim
  does not reduce to the first two merely because humans encounter it
  through archetypal imagery. **Archetype–Ontology Bifurcation** is
  this trail's named move (Idea Trail tier — never an operator
  candidate).
- **Seed:** SWEEP_14's closing branch — God as highest-order moral
  symbol vs God as ground of goodness, inside the Jungian/theological
  overlap.
- **Kinship:** IT-101 (the terminus question — ground-of-goodness is
  its moral face); IT-104 (interface models); IT-097 (choosing belief
  vs practice); Family 8's internal/external/metanormative critique
  three-way.
- **Unfinished:** the relation between symbol, interface, and
  ontological source has never been argued in a bounded thread — only
  gestured at; what evidence would distinguish a regulator from an
  interface is exactly IT-104's question wearing theological clothes.
