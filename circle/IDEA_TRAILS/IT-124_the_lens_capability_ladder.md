# IT-124 — The Lens-Capability Ladder

**Status:** OPEN (Idea Trail)
**Registry:** #124 · **Opened:** 2026-07-20 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)

- **Core instrument (SW19's centerpiece):** possessed → accessible →
  salient → recognized as relevant → selectable → competently
  deployable → comparable against rivals → revisable/suppressible →
  acquirable. **Possession is not access; access is not salience;
  salience is not selection; selection is not competent deployment.**
- **Seed:** the "available lenses" dispute — Grant's weak reported
  thesis (*one cannot deploy a wholly absent capacity*) is nearly
  tautological; the live challenge sits higher on the ladder (*why
  isn't a demonstrably possessed context-sensitive lens selected when
  literal analysis keeps failing the speaker-fidelity test?*). Direct
  Ziggy support covers only possession/access/selection; the full
  nine rungs are dig-level completion architecture, never his
  explicit theory (the tier discipline held across two Stage-4
  patches).
- **The unknown-vs-refusal guard (binding, minted at Stage 3):**
  *alternative-lens availability UNKNOWN* is the safe finding —
  never *"possessed and voluntarily refused"* without direct
  evidence of the refusal.
- **Kinship:** IT-093/096/118 (the possibility and target ladders —
  this is their capability-side cousin); IT-121 (the interpretive
  target ledger — method selection is one of its objects); Method-
  Trade-Off Audit (SW18 — grant the function, name the cost, applied
  here to lens selection specifically).
- **Unfinished:** the Lens-State Evidence and Unknown-versus-Refusal
