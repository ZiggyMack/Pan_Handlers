# IT-127 — Asymmetric Grounding and the Opposite-Compatibility Demand

**Status:** OPEN (Idea Trail)
**Registry:** #127 · **Opened:** 2026-07-20 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)

- **Core finding — Grant's B1/B2 challenge (G-QUOTED), ruled SWEEP_20's
  philosophical center:** *if a proposed ground is equally compatible
  with an explanandum and its negation, it has not yet been shown to
  explain why the explanandum obtains.* Repaired across all four
  stages from an initial misreading as a blanket deduction-only demand
  into its true, narrower, and stronger form: explanation need not be
  strict entailment, but mere coexistence of ground and explanandum
  does not establish that the ground explains it. The acceptable
  bridge may be entailment, metaphysical necessitation, constitution,
  causal production, probabilistic support, or another non-arbitrary
  asymmetric relation — but *some* such relation must be shown.
- **The dig's decisive unresolved point (held as an A2 omission
  through Stage 3, never closed):** neither Ziggy nor Nova ever
  actually built the asymmetric B1–B2 bridge. Rejecting deductive
  entailment as the required form only widened the menu of acceptable
  bridge-types — it did not supply one. This is the trail's live
  question.
- **A1/B1/B2 held UNKNOWN throughout:** the Stage-2 repair (SW20)
  refused to supply substantive definitions ("A1 = X is brute," "B1 =
  God is brute," "B2 = X is a feature of God") absent Grant's own
  explicit assignment — a source-discipline holding worth naming
  alongside the demand itself, since the demand's force depends on
  what A1/B1/B2 actually denote.
