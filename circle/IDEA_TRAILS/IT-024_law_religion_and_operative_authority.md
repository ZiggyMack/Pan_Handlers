# IT-024 — Law, Religion, and Operative Authority

**Status:** OPEN
**Registry:** #24 · **Opened:** 2026-07-17 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)
*(Field-desk id at ordering: "IT-025" — recurring off-by-one; registry id is canonical.)*

- **Core trajectory:** agreed game → inherited jurisdiction → coercive
  compliance → present rigidity → enforcement invariant → source/access
  distinction → fallible collective handling.
- **Seed:** COMPOUND_SWEEP_05's law/religion branch — the field desk rated it
  HIGH-YIELD, HIGH-DISANALOGY RISK, and the sweep's *most philosophically
  novel* material.
- **The two strongest independent findings:**
  1. **Operative-Rigidity Test** — a rule can be diachronically revisable yet
     synchronically fixed for the person under judgment; abstract
     institutional provisionality vs lived coercion.
  2. **Claimed-Source / Claimed-Access Separation** — *an objective good
     exists ≠ a human agent possesses it infallibly ≠ that agent may enforce
     its interpretation without correction* (Representation ≠ Ontology
     adjacency).
- **Unlocking distinction:** compliance ≠ endorsement ≠ consent ≠
  legitimation — prudential obedience under coercion licenses none of the
  others; this dissolved the claimed inconsistency between rejecting divine
  authority and obeying civil law.
- **Registered boundary:** law and religion are comparable ONLY under
  explicitly selected variables (inherited authority, command, sanction,
  present coercion) — total equivalence rejected at every stage; the
  variable-by-variable comparison table (source, access, consent,
  revisability, appeal, jurisdiction, accountability, epistemic claim) is the
  trail's method contribution.
- **Branches:** IT-019 (justificatory jurisdiction — the authority problem);
  Symmetric-Wielding Proposal (procedural ledger, not a cognitive operator).
- **Unfinished:** the missing inconsistency evidence (a secular participant

  of law's and religion's distinct social/metaphysical roles (downgraded to
  A1 — never demonstrated).
