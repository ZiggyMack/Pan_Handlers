# IT-131 — Auditor Convergence Is Not Truth

**Status:** OPEN (Idea Trail)
**Registry:** #131 · **Opened:** 2026-07-21 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)

- **Core finding — SWEEP_22, the most dangerous untyped claim in the
  packet:** Ziggy reports that a system (CFA, his broader project
  ecosystem) *seeks* approximately 98% convergence among multiple or
  representative auditors as a governance safeguard against
  ideological capture. The repair's central move: the source
  establishes only that the system is **looking for** ~98%
  convergence — a target or design criterion, not an achieved,
  independently verified result. Convergence itself is radically
  under-typed (verdict agreement / score agreement / ranking agreement
  / repeated-run consistency / confidence overlap / output or semantic
  similarity / agreement reached only after auditors interact).
- **The load-bearing separations:** **convergence ≠ truth**;
  **diversity ≠ independence**; **multiple auditors ≠ multiple
  epistemic lineages.** Auditors can converge because they share base
  models, training data, prompts, ontology, objectives, reward
  signals, or benchmark contamination — correlated error dressed as
  validation. The needed instrument is a **common-mode-failure audit**:
  auditor count, surface diversity, causal independence, epistemic
  diversity, error correlation, calibration, ground-truth performance,
  adversarial robustness, minority-dissent preservation, and
  governance over auditor selection/weighting. A unanimous jury cloned
  from the same mistaken template is not pluralism.
- **The counter-intuitive risk this repair surfaced:** a *high*
  convergence threshold can actively **suppress** the dissent needed
  for correction — pressuring conformity, discarding minority
  auditors, rewarding lowest-common-denominator judgments, concealing
  uncertainty, and privileging whichever ontology happens to be
  dominant. "Destructive interference" (the reported reason for not
  running incompatible systems together) needs its own operational
  definition — logical inconsistency, incompatible ontology, prompt
  contamination, feedback instability, or genuine adversarial testing
  are not the same failure, and conflating them risks turning
  legitimate falsification attempts into something to be siloed away.
- **Kinship:** IT-014 (invariance, stable structure, and model
  convergence — a different domain, worth checking for the same
  convergence-≠-truth shape); the detector-vs-disease family (IT-126)
  — here the "safeguard" under scrutiny is the convergence design
  itself, not a registry-mapping error, but the discipline is the
  same: don't let a control's existence stand in for its proven
  effectiveness.
- **Unfinished:** what 98% actually ranges over (which claims, which

  minority dissent is preserved or discarded; whether this finding
  generalizes to Ziggy's other named frameworks in the same conceptual
  ecosystem (Nyquist Consciousness) if and when they become dig
  subjects.
