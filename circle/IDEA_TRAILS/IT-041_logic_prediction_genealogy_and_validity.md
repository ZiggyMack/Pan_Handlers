# IT-041 — Logic, Prediction, Genealogy, and Validity

**Status:** OPEN
**Registry:** #41 · **Opened:** 2026-07-17 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)
*(Field-desk id at ordering: "IT-042" — recurring off-by-one; registry id is canonical.)*

- **Core trajectory:** anticipatory behavior → evolved cognition → logical
  formalization → prediction testing → justificatory circularity.
- **Seed:** DIG_AUX_07's dependency dispute — "does logic come from
  prediction, or prediction from logic?" ruled too crude: **six priority
  types** now govern (conceptual / psychological / evolutionary /
  computational / justificatory / metaphysical).
- **The mixed answer (stronger than any ladder):** primitive anticipation
  may be psychologically and evolutionarily prior; logical distinctions
  are conceptually required to formulate and score explicit predictions;
  predictive reliability may help justify inferential confidence; none of
  it establishes metaphysical sovereignty.
- **Unlocking distinction — Origin ≠ Validity:** *why logical cognition
  evolved ≠ what makes an inference valid* — blocks the genetic fallacy
  without refuting naturalized epistemology (the live reply: *reliability,
  adaptive success, and world-responsive functioning may be part of what
  justification consists in*).
- **The predictive-processing counter (preserved):** the logical relations
  prediction presupposes might be operations *within* the predictive
  system — blocking any easy one-way hierarchy without erasing the
  conceptual distinctions.
- **Branches:** IT-040 (sovereignty); IT-042 (reflexive standards); the
  "delete_14" titled lead (~06-08 — this exact question in an earlier
  thread; natural companion dig).
- **Unfinished:** the definition of prediction (seven senses); whether
  logic's authority is constitutive, pragmatic, foundational, or
  reciprocal; an extractor-independent instance of the dependency
  analysis.
