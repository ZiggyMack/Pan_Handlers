# IT-095 — Credence, Trust, Reliance, and Commitment

**Status:** OPEN (Idea Trail)
**Registry:** #95 · **Opened:** 2026-07-17 · **Observers:** Nova (field-desk), P.H. Claude (filing)
*(Field-desk id at ordering: "IT-098" — numbering drift; registry id is canonical.)*

- **Core question:** which attitude actually does the work that
  "faith" is credited with? The candidate ledger: credence (graded),
  categorical belief, working belief, trust, reliance, practical
  commitment, policy, risk acceptance, hope.
- **Seed:** SWEEP_13's faith sense ledger (six senses; neither side
  wins by controlling the dictionary) plus the semantic-cost argument
  — Grant's strongest reconstruction includes that calling ordinary
  uncertain action "faith" may import religious connotations without
  adding explanatory value.
- **The explanatory-gain audit (confirmed A3-11, later partially
  repaired in-thread):** what gain does "faith" provide? what
  misunderstanding does it predictably cause? could *trust, reliance,
  commitment, policy,* or *risk acceptance* perform the same work more
  clearly? Nova's late concession: if the underlying phenomenon is
  accepted, consider conceding the label.
- **Unlocking distinction:** working reliance ≠ categorical belief —
  one can act on a proposition one does not believe (and vice versa);
  a decision rule is not automatically faith.
- **Kinship:** IT-094 (thresholds); IT-093 (trivialization); the
  standing "Trust vs Prediction" lead (8 pointers — this trail's
  likely future collision partner).
- **Unfinished:** a cost-benefit scoring of retaining vs conceding a
  contested word (candidate general instrument — semantic escalation
  loops recur across the circle).
