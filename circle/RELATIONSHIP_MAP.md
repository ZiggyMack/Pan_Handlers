## What This Map Is (and Is Not)

Not a "who agrees with whom" graph. The unit of analysis is the **operator
collision**: which cognitive move meets which, where translation succeeds, where
standards conflict, and which pairings generate new ideas.

## Planned Structures

1. **Operator complementarity matrix** — members × members, cells list the
   recurring CO-### collisions (e.g., the predicted CO-005 Possibility Compression
   vs. possibility-preservation collision between Grant and Ziggy).
2. **Recurring disagreement map** — disputes that re-arise across debates, with
   links into `DEBATES/` records and both members' `POSITION_LEDGER.md`.
3. **Idea-origin graph** — who seeded what, feeding `IDEA_TRAILS/`.
4. **Argument lineage graph** — how specific arguments mutated across sessions.
5. **Unresolved debate registry** — open disputes with their current dialectical
   state (remember: contested ≠ defeated).
6. **Translation-failure map** — where members systematically talk past each
   other, and which reframings have historically repaired it.
7. **Trust and repair history** — ruptures and the mechanisms that mended them
   (intellectual dynamics only; no gossip — `ETHICS_AND_CONSENT.md` §3).

## Entry Discipline

Every edge in every graph cites: the debate record(s) it derives from, the
observer who drew the edge, and a confidence label. Edges are claims and obey the
same rules as all claims (`PROFILE_SCHEMA.md` §1).
