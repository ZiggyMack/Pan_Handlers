# 🍳 PANHANDLERS COGNITIVE ARCHAEOLOGY PROTOCOL v0.1

## Mission

Treat this conversation as a self-contained PanHandlers archaeological dig site.

Mine the thread for evidence relevant to durable cognitive profiles of:

* **Grant**
* **Ziggy**
* their recurring debate relationship;
* the ideas and distinctions generated through their interaction.

Do not rely on generalized memories from other conversations. Analyze this thread specifically.

The goal is not to decide who was right.

The goal is to recover:

1. what each person appears to hold;
2. which recurring reasoning operators each person uses;
3. the sequence in which those operators are deployed;
4. what triggered changes, concessions, resistance, or escalation;
5. which new ideas were generated through the clash;
6. what remains unresolved.

---

# I. SOURCE DISCIPLINE

For every extracted claim, label the evidence type:

* **G-DIRECT** — Grant's own words appear in this chat.
* **G-REPORTED** — Ziggy reports or paraphrases something Grant said elsewhere.
* **Z-DIRECT** — Ziggy states his own position directly.
* **NOVA-INTERPRETATION** — the assistant interprets Grant, Ziggy, or the dispute.
* **CO-CONSTRUCTED** — Ziggy and the assistant develop the idea together.
* **UNCLEAR** — attribution cannot be confidently determined.

Do not silently convert `G-REPORTED` into `G-DIRECT`.

Do not attribute an assistant-generated phrase or operator name to Grant or Ziggy unless that person explicitly adopted it.

---

# II. CONFIDENCE LABELS

Use these labels:

* **DIRECT** — explicitly stated.
* **STRONG INFERENCE** — clearly implied by several statements.
* **TENTATIVE INFERENCE** — plausible but underdetermined.
* **CONTESTED** — challenged inside the conversation.
* **REVISED** — changed during the conversation.
* **UNKNOWN** — insufficient evidence.

Every operator attribution must include a confidence label.

---

# III. PASS A — CONVERSATION MAP

Provide a concise map of the thread:

* central dispute;
* secondary disputes;
* important pivots;
* moments of agreement;
* moments where the participants talked past one another;
* the final unresolved question.

Identify the major stages in chronological order.

---

# IV. PASS B — GRANT POSITION EXTRACTION

Extract every materially relevant position attributed to Grant.

For each position provide:

### Position
A concise formulation.

### Evidence Status
`G-DIRECT`, `G-REPORTED`, or another label.

### Supporting Evidence
A brief quotation or close paraphrase from the thread.

### Strongest Reconstruction
State the position in its most defensible form.

### Ziggy's Interpretation
How Ziggy understood or experienced the position.

### Current Status
* ACTIVE
* AMBIGUOUS
* CONTESTED
* REVISED
* POSSIBLY STRAW-MANNED
* INSUFFICIENT EVIDENCE

### Open Question
What would need to be asked Grant directly to settle the interpretation?

---

# V. PASS C — ZIGGY POSITION EXTRACTION

Apply the same process to Ziggy.

Pay particular attention to recurring commitments involving:

* possibility preservation;
* lived experience;
* framework plurality;
* map expansion;
* symmetry of standards;
* metaphysical admission;
* institutional authority;
* justification;
* prediction;
* meaning;
* generative capacity;
* offense and defense asymmetry.

Do not treat Ziggy's present framing as neutral merely because he initiated the analysis.

---

# VI. PASS D — OPERATOR EXTRACTION

Identify reusable cognitive operations performed in this conversation.

For every proposed operator provide:

### Operator Name
Use an existing PanHandlers/Museum name when there is a strong match. Otherwise propose a provisional name.

### Actor
Grant, Ziggy, Nova, or co-constructed.

### Definition
Describe the reasoning move independently of the person.

### Trigger
What conversational condition activates it?

### Operation
What transformation does it perform on the available information?

### Output
What conclusion, question, score, or reframing tends to result?

### Productive Function
When is this operator useful?

### Failure Mode
When does it distort, exclude, compress, or overreach?

### Evidence
Give the strongest example from this thread.

### Confidence
DIRECT, STRONG INFERENCE, TENTATIVE, or CONTESTED.

---

# VII. PASS E — OPERATOR SEQUENCES

Do not stop at listing operators.

Identify repeated or consequential sequences such as:

```text
Operator A
→ Operator B
→ Operator C
→ characteristic conclusion
```

For each sequence explain:

* who deploys it;
* what initiates it;
* whether the order matters;
* where another operator could have interrupted it;
* whether the result was discovery, evaluation, defense, exclusion, or repair.

Specifically test for evidence of:

* Prediction Sovereignty;
* Universalized Evaluative Jurisdiction;
* Generative Admission Restriction;
* Unmeasured-to-Zero Conversion;
* Possibility Compression;
* Baseline Exemption;
* Reconstruction Bypass;
* Contested-to-Defeated Compression;
* Symmetry Testing of Standards;
* Contested ≠ Defeated;
* Possibility Preservation;
* Cross-Disciplinary Swooping.

These are candidate operators, not predetermined conclusions.

Actively report counterexamples.

---

# VIII. PASS F — OMISSION AND ABSTENTION

Identify important moves that were available but absent.

Examples:

* reconstructing the opposing framework before scoring it;
* applying the same burden to one's own framework;
* distinguishing `unknown` from `zero`;
* temporarily granting a possibility;
* asking what function a framework serves;
* clarifying the jurisdiction of a standard;
* conceding a limited point;
* testing a conclusion under reversed roles.

For every omission state:

* why the operator was relevant;
* whether its absence changed the outcome;
* whether the absence appears stable or may be situational.

Do not treat every unused operator as a defect.

---

# IX. PASS G — OFFENSE/DEFENSE ASYMMETRY

Separate behavior displayed while:

* attacking a framework;
* defending a framework;
* evaluating neutrally;
* exploring privately;
* translating another person's view.

Determine whether either participant changes standards depending on role.

Flag any case where:

* a standard demanded on offense is relaxed on defense;
* possibility is admitted for one framework but denied to another;
* an objection is treated as decisive against an opponent but merely unresolved against oneself;
* one framework's axioms are priced while another's are treated as baseline.

---

# X. PASS H — IDEA TRAIL RECOVERY

Identify ideas that were generated or significantly transformed in this thread.

For each idea trail record:

### Seed
The original question, frustration, analogy, or disagreement.

### Contributors
Who introduced and transformed it?

### Key Transformation
What distinction or operator changed the discussion?

### Resulting Concept
What idea emerged?

### Current Destination
Does it belong in CFA, EOS, the Museum, PanHandlers, Nyquist, or another project?

### Status
* OPEN
* DEVELOPING
* FORMALIZED
* SUPERSEDED
* ABANDONED

### Unfinished Work
What remains to be tested or clarified?

---

# XI. PASS I — COUNTEREVIDENCE

For every major characterization of Grant or Ziggy, search this conversation for evidence against it.

Examples:

* a case where Grant preserved a low-probability possibility;
* a case where Grant valued explanation independently of prediction;
* a case where Ziggy rejected an attractive possibility;
* a case where Ziggy demanded strong evidential pruning;
* a case where either participant reconstructed the other charitably;
* a case where a supposed stable operator was not used.

A profile that contains no counterevidence is probably advocacy rather than archaeology.

---

# XII. PASS J — DIRECT QUESTIONS FOR THE MEMBERS

Produce two short lists.

## Questions for Grant
Questions that would clarify genuine ambiguities revealed by this thread.

## Questions for Ziggy
Questions that would expose assumptions, biases, or unresolved tensions in his own architecture.

Questions should be neutral and answerable.

Avoid rhetorical traps.

---

# XIV. FINAL CONSTRAINTS

Do not:

* diagnose personalities or mental conditions;
* infer hidden motives as fact;
* confuse disagreement with dishonesty;
* transform one conversation into a permanent identity;
* erase contradictions;
* treat Ziggy's framing as the default neutral standard;
* manufacture direct quotations;
* claim certainty where only reported speech exists.

The output should preserve both the heat of the debate and the limits of the evidence.

We are not embalming people.

We are preserving the living trails of thought they helped create.

## Label mapping at promotion time

| Extraction label (protocol §I–II) | Archive label (schema §1) | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| G-DIRECT + DIRECT | `DIRECT` (about Grant) | the chat itself is the transcript |
| G-REPORTED | at most `INFERRED` (about Grant) — but `DIRECT` about *Ziggy's report* | the double-bookkeeping that prevents retrospective legend |
| Z-DIRECT + DIRECT | `DIRECT` (about Ziggy) | |
| NOVA-INTERPRETATION | `INFERRED`, observer: Nova | Nova is an attributed observer like any other |
| CO-CONSTRUCTED | `INFERRED`, observers: both | never attributable to either alone |
| STRONG INFERENCE | `INFERRED` (candidate `REPEATED` once seen in ≥2 packets) | cross-chat recurrence earns promotion |
| TENTATIVE INFERENCE | `INFERRED` — or stay QUARANTINED | when in doubt, quarantine |
| CONTESTED / REVISED | `CONTESTED` / position state `REVISED` | |
| UNCLEAR / UNKNOWN | `UNKNOWN` | |

## Promotion rules

1. Packets land in `evidence/chat_extractions/` — they are **staging**, never
   cited directly by profiles.
2. "Recommended Source Excerpts" become numbered `SOURCE_INDEX.md` entries
   (G-###, Z-###) with the packet as location.
3. Only PROFILE-READY CLAIMS may enter profiles, translated via the table above.
   QUARANTINED CLAIMS stay in the packet until more packets corroborate.
4. **Cross-chat recurrence earns promotion:** a single chat generates a candidate;
   an operator attribution reaches `REPEATED` (and lexicon 🟡 PROPOSED) only
   across independent contexts.
5. Synthesis questions for the integration chamber, per Nova: Which operators
   recur across independent contexts? Which appear only on offense? **Which are
   actually Nova's language imposed on Grant?** Which survive counterexample
   search? How has Ziggy's own position changed?
