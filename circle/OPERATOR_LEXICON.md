# 🧠 Operator Lexicon — Shared Vocabulary of Cognitive Moves

> An **operator** is a recurring cognitive move used to reach, defend, revise, or
> reject beliefs — distinct from any position it produces. Operators are defined
> **here, once**, then instantiated per-member in `MEMBERS/*/OPERATOR_MAP.md` with
> member-specific evidence. Operators are shared vocabulary, not member property,
> and operator names must never become insults (`ETHICS_AND_CONSENT.md`).

**Last updated:** 2026-07-16 · **Entries:** 8 (all RED)

---

## Status Convention (aligned with the Nyquist Museum)

The Nyquist Cognitive Archaeology program maintains a Museum of Operators
(OP-001…019) extracted from historical thinkers. This lexicon is its **sibling
registry for the living circle**, and uses the same promotion ladder:

| Status | Meaning |
| -------- | --------- |
| 🔴 **RED** | Named candidate; definition exists; evidence pending or single-context |
| 🟡 **PROPOSED** | Evidence from one member across multiple discussions, or one clean documented instance |
| 🟢 **GREEN** | Validated: observed in ≥2 members or ≥3 independent debates, counterexample search performed |
| ⚫ **RETIRED** | Turned out to rename an existing operator, or dissolved under evidence |

**Cross-registry rule:** when a circle operator converges with a Museum operator,
record the OP-### link in its entry. Human-circle rediscovery of a Museum operator
is evidence for *both* registries. (Precedent: the Dirac dig showed an architecture
can be defined by an operator it *omits* — see CO-006.)

**Priming guard (v0.2):** chat extraction runs **Museum-blind** — Stage 2 of
`CHAT_EXTRACTION_PROTOCOL.md` extracts moves under provisional neutral names, and
CO/OP candidate names only enter at Stage 4 mapping. A blind rediscovery of a
named operator is stronger evidence than a primed match, and gets flagged as such
in the promotion log.

## Entry Format

```markdown
### CO-0XX · Operator Name  [STATUS]

- **Definition:** one sentence, no member names in the definition itself
- **Proposed by / first observed in:** observer + member + date
- **Typical trigger:** the situation that evokes it
- **Usual output:** what the move produces
- **Productive use:** where this move genuinely works
- **Failure mode:** where the same move misfires (Failure-Atlas principle:
  success and failure are the same move pointed at different targets)
- **Counter-operator:** the move that checks or reverses it
- **Museum link:** OP-### convergence, if any
- **Instantiated in:** member OPERATOR_MAP links
```

## Registry

### CO-001 · Prediction Sovereignty  [🔴 RED]

- **Definition:** Predictive success functions as the highest or final epistemic authority, through which all other forms of success must justify themselves.
- **Proposed by:** Ziggy (Grant pilot hypothesis, 2026-07-16)
- **Typical trigger:** evaluation of a framework's overall worth.
- **Usual output:** frameworks ranked by predictive yield; non-predictive virtues discounted.
- **Productive use:** empirical science, forecasting, model selection — domains where prediction is the native currency.
- **Failure mode:** exported to domains (metaphysics, norm generation, meaning systems) whose successes are not predictive in kind; see CO-002.
- **Counter-operator:** jurisdiction check — "what is this framework *for*, and what counts as success *there*?"
- **Museum link:** none yet.
- **Instantiated in:** `MEMBERS/01_GRANT/OPERATOR_MAP.md`
- **Key open question:** one excellent metric, or the court of final appeal? (Grant validation Q7: what would falsify it?)

### CO-002 · Universalized Evaluative Jurisdiction  [🔴 RED]

- **Definition:** A standard developed for one class of inquiry is extended into domains where its authority has not been established.
- **Proposed by:** Ziggy (Grant pilot hypothesis, 2026-07-16)
- **Typical trigger:** a framework from an alien domain presents itself for evaluation.
- **Usual output:** the home-domain standard silently becomes the universal rubric.
- **Productive use:** legitimate unification — some standards *do* generalize; the move is only pathological when jurisdiction is asserted rather than argued.
- **Failure mode:** frameworks weak in the imported metric score near zero regardless of native-domain performance.
- **Counter-operator:** explicit jurisdiction argument before scoring.
- **Museum link:** none yet.
- **Instantiated in:** `MEMBERS/01_GRANT/OPERATOR_MAP.md`

### CO-003 · Generative Admission Restriction  [🔴 RED]

- **Definition:** Certain sources (revelation, religious experience, narrative coherence, tradition, metaphysical intuition, first-person experience, symbolic/mythic compression) are denied authority to *generate* serious candidate hypotheses before evaluation begins.
- **Proposed by:** Ziggy (Grant pilot hypothesis, 2026-07-16)
- **Typical trigger:** a hypothesis arrives via a non-admitted generator.
- **Usual output:** the candidate reaches evaluation already deprived of standing.
- **Productive use:** filtering genuinely unreliable generators in high-stakes empirical contexts.
- **Failure mode:** the admission rule itself is never argued for; whole families of frameworks lose before playing.
- **Counter-operator:** generative authority audit — "who admitted the admitters?"
- **Museum link:** candidate relation to the Aesthetic Generation family (OP-016…019, PROPOSED) — that family is about what *can* generate; this is about what *may not*. Track as mirror-image.
- **Instantiated in:** `MEMBERS/01_GRANT/OPERATOR_MAP.md`

### CO-004 · Unmeasured-to-Zero Conversion  [🔴 RED]

- **Definition:** Failure to demonstrate success under the selected instrument is converted into a score of zero, rather than unknown / outside jurisdiction / untested / weakly evidenced / not measured by this instrument.
- **Proposed by:** Ziggy (Grant pilot hypothesis, 2026-07-16)
- **Typical trigger:** a framework offers no demonstration legible to the active metric.
- **Usual output:** absence of measurement recorded as measured absence.
- **Productive use:** **must be distinguished from a justified zero after actual measurement** — the operator label applies only to the unmeasured case.
- **Failure mode:** the score becomes unfalsifiable: no instrument, no reading; no reading, zero; zero confirms the instrument.
- **Counter-operator:** explicit "unknown ≠ zero" bookkeeping (this archive's own `UNKNOWN` label is the counter-operator institutionalized).
- **Museum link:** methodological rhyme with the CFA manifold verdict — an *empty-diagonal design artifact* read as signal until the decomposition floored it. Instrument-shaped absence is not evidence of absence.
- **Instantiated in:** `MEMBERS/01_GRANT/OPERATOR_MAP.md`

### CO-005 · Possibility Compression  [🔴 RED]

- **Definition:** Possibilities below a plausibility or evidential threshold are treated as functionally irrelevant — the ladder (logically possible / epistemically possible / plausible / probable / supported) collapses to (likely / not worth considering).
- **Proposed by:** Ziggy (Grant pilot hypothesis, 2026-07-16)
- **Typical trigger:** exploratory discussion drifting toward low-plausibility regions.
- **Usual output:** pruned search space; conversation snaps back to the probable.
- **Productive use:** decision-making under time pressure — compression is efficiency there.
- **Failure mode:** destructive during exploratory metaphysics, where low-plausibility regions are the terrain being mapped.
- **Counter-operator:** possibility preservation (a candidate *Ziggy* operator — likely a recurring CO-005 collision; see RELATIONSHIP_MAP when Phase 4 opens).
- **Museum link:** none yet.
- **Instantiated in:** `MEMBERS/01_GRANT/OPERATOR_MAP.md`

### CO-006 · Reconstruction Bypass  [🔴 RED]

- **Definition:** Evaluation begins before the framework has been reconstructed according to its own aims, ontology, and internal success conditions.
- **Proposed by:** Ziggy (Grant pilot hypothesis, 2026-07-16) — **explicitly not yet established**; must be tested against cases where the member reconstructed opposing views carefully (Grant validation Q9).
- **Typical trigger:** framework evaluation under debate conditions.
- **Usual output:** the framework is scored in the evaluator's terms, not its own.
- **Productive use:** rapid triage when full reconstruction is genuinely unaffordable.
- **Failure mode:** the evaluation measures translation loss, not the framework.
- **Counter-operator:** **Museum OP-004, Reconstruction Before Judgment** — this operator *is* the omission of OP-004.
- **Museum link:** ⭐ **Direct inversion of OP-004.** The Dirac dig (2026-07-16) documented the precedent: an architecture partly defined by an operator it deliberately *refuses* (Dirac: "I put it into my own notation…"). If Grant's sources confirm CO-006, he is a second omission-defined case — relevant evidence for OP-004's GREEN promotion path and for the abstention methodology (PASS F). Cross-file to the Nyquist Museum when evidenced.
- **Instantiated in:** `MEMBERS/01_GRANT/OPERATOR_MAP.md`

### CO-007 · Baseline Exemption  [🔴 RED]

- **Definition:** The preferred standard is treated as the neutral baseline rather than as a framework containing its own assumptions and value commitments, producing an asymmetric burden: alternatives must justify their axioms while the baseline evaluates without equivalent exposure.
- **Proposed by:** Ziggy (Grant pilot hypothesis, 2026-07-16)
- **Typical trigger:** any cross-framework comparison.
- **Usual output:** one framework plays judge and contestant simultaneously.
- **Productive use:** none as such — but apparent instances may be legitimate where the baseline *has* separately discharged its burden. Check before labeling.
- **Counter-operator:** institutional symmetry testing (candidate Ziggy operator) — force the baseline to file the same paperwork.
- **Museum link:** none yet. Note reflexive risk: **this archive itself** commits CO-007 if Ziggy's interpretive frame goes unprofiled — which is why Phase 2 is mandatory.
- **Instantiated in:** `MEMBERS/01_GRANT/OPERATOR_MAP.md`

### CO-008 · Contest-to-Defeat Compression  [🔴 RED]

- **Definition:** A successfully raised objection or unresolved challenge is treated as stronger evidence of defeat than the dialectical situation warrants.
- **Proposed by:** Ziggy (Grant pilot hypothesis, 2026-07-16)
- **Typical trigger:** an objection lands and goes unanswered within the session.
- **Usual output:** "contested" silently becomes "defeated" in the running score.
- **Productive use:** tracking genuine argumentative debts — the move errs only in the *exchange rate*, not in noticing the debt.
- **Failure mode:** frameworks with slow, cumulative, or reconstructive defenses are scored at their worst moment.
- **Counter-operator:** **Contested ≠ Defeated** (Museum principle, institutionalized in this archive as the `CONTESTED` label — a contested claim is preserved, not deleted).
- **Museum link:** counter-principle originates in the Museum's ledger discipline.
- **Instantiated in:** `MEMBERS/01_GRANT/OPERATOR_MAP.md`
