## Topic clusters — candidates for combination

Same underlying question asked more than once, in roughly chronological
groups. These are where "do we need all of these as separate topics" is a
real question.

- **Foundations & regress** — `AUX_07` (faith as axiomatic bedrock) →
  `SW03` (jurisdiction of justification) → `SW20` (brute fact/grounding,
  God's "free" exemption) → `SW21` (the higher-reason regress). Same
  question asked four times with an increasingly sharp instrument: *what,
  if anything, legitimately stops a justification chain, and does the
  stopping point get exempted from its own rule?*
- **Master frame / ultimate value** — `SW04` and `SW05` are an explicit
  direct sequel pair asking whether any single value (prediction, truth,
  suffering, law, religion, consciousness) can be sovereign. Strongest
  single merge candidate on the board — same question, same conclusion
  each time (no single master recovered).
- **Myth as compression** — `AUX_06` (micro) → `SW07` (its named sequel,
  also branching off SW05). Both ask whether myths function like Shannon
  compression and whether interpretive disagreement defeats that claim.
- **Possibility vs. plausibility / evidential threshold** — `SW02`, `SW08`
  (belief rescue), `SW09` (agnostic priors, miracle claims). All three ask
  when a merely-possible claim earns the right to be believed or acted on.
- **The Grant-direct formal-logic era** — `SW12` (lying ladder), `SW13`
  (faith-as-action), `SW14` (protection/defense), `SW16`
  (divine-command formalization), `SW17` (decision/credence). Different
  surface topics, same method each time: take one specific Grant claim and
  build a graduated ladder under it. Worth treating as one methodological
  arc even though the six topics themselves stay distinct.
- **Interpretation & attribution** — `SW15` (straw-manning ledger) →
  `SW18` (Grant interprets Tale) → `SW19` (available lenses / starting
  whole). All three ask when reading implications into someone's words
  becomes misattribution rather than analysis.
- **Angles / power & governance** — `SW10` (noise as participation-cost)
  and `SW22` (archetypal frame mobility, power/legitimacy, auditor
  convergence) are the only two digs that reach outside the
  Grant-vs-Ziggy dyad into governance-shaped questions. Worth reading as
  a pair even though Angles is only directly present in one of them.
- **The Tale Recursion arc** — `AUX_05` (first appearance) → `SW18`
  (interpreted party in the Grant dispute) → `SW19` (repeated
  corrections). A real chronological character arc across three digs,
  the only one of its kind on the board.
- **The SW16 quartet** — Tom, Sorta, Kee, and Tapioca all enter and exit
  in a single dig. No clustering needed, but it's the only place all four
  co-occur, which is worth remembering if any of them get a formal
  membership decision.
