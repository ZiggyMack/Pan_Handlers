# I. DESIGN PRINCIPLES

## 1. Profile the Reasoning, Not the Soul

A profile describes observable patterns in a member's arguments and interactions.

It must distinguish:

- what the member explicitly said;
- what has been repeatedly observed;
- what is inferred;
- what remains uncertain;
- what may be situational rather than stable.

Do not turn temporary behavior into permanent identity.

## 2. Every Claim Gets a Confidence Level

- **DIRECT** — explicitly stated by the member.
- **REPEATED** — observed across several discussions.
- **INFERRED** — explanatory hypothesis supported by evidence.
- **CONTESTED** — disputed by the member or another observer.
- **STALE** — may no longer represent the member.
- **UNKNOWN** — not enough evidence.

## 3. Separate Position from Operator

A **position** is what someone believes.
An **operator** is the recurring cognitive move used to reach, defend, revise, or reject beliefs.

Example:

- Position: "Prediction is the strongest epistemic standard."
- Operator: **Prediction Sovereignty** — treating predictive success as the final court of appeal across multiple domains.

Positions may change while operators remain stable.

## 4. Preserve Contradictions

Contradictions are not automatically defects to erase. They may reveal:

- domain-dependent standards;
- developmental changes;
- unresolved tensions;
- different offense and defense behavior;
- hidden priority orderings;
- framework drift.

Contradictions are recorded as active research questions.

## 5. Profiles Must Remain Revisable

Every profile includes: version number; last review date; contributing observers;
unresolved objections; revision history; a member-response section.

No profile becomes canonical merely because it was written first.

# III. PROFILE SCHEMA

See `PROFILE_SCHEMA.md` for the operational contract. Sections A–L:

- **A. Orientation** — name/handle, role, relationship to project, domains, version, freshness notice.
- **B. Self-Declared Commitments** — only statements the person has explicitly endorsed.
- **C. Observed Cognitive Strengths** — each with evidence and failure conditions.
- **D. Recurring Operators** — name, definition, evidence, trigger, output, productive use, failure mode, counter-operator, confidence.
- **E. Operator Sequences** — order of deployment; hypotheses until validated across multiple debates.
- **F. Position Ledger** — original formulation, strongest reconstruction, revisions, status, confidence, debates, objections. States: ACTIVE / REVISED / ABANDONED / AMBIGUOUS / CONTESTED / UNKNOWN.
- **G. Idea Trails** — seed, introducer, transformers, unlocking distinctions, branches, unfinished work, inheriting projects.
- **H. Debate Behavior** — offense / defense / neutral analysis / private reflection / group / one-on-one, kept separate.
- **I. Productive Tensions** — recorded without premature resolution.
- **J. Relationship Dynamics** — intellectual interactions, not gossip.
- **K. Open Questions** — what the profile cannot yet answer.
- **L. Member Response** — endorse / reject / revise / annotate / supply context / challenge labels. The archive records both profile and response.

# IV. GRANT AS THE PILOT PROFILE

Grant is developed first because his architecture is already partially visible across
repeated discussions. The purpose is not to prove why Grant is "wrong." The purpose is
to explain why his evaluations remain stable even after extensive argument.

## Initial Grant Operator Candidates

1. **Prediction Sovereignty** — Predictive success functions as the highest or final
   epistemic authority. Key question: one excellent metric, or the standard through
   which all other forms of success must justify themselves?

2. **Universalized Evaluative Jurisdiction** — A standard developed for one class of
   inquiry is extended into domains where its authority has not yet been established.

3. **Generative Admission Restriction** — Certain sources are denied authority to
   generate serious candidate hypotheses before evaluation begins (revelation,
   religious experience, narrative coherence, tradition, metaphysical intuition,
   first-person experience, symbolic/mythic compression).

4. **Unmeasured-to-Zero Conversion** — Failure to demonstrate success under the
   selected instrument is converted into a score of zero rather than: unknown /
   outside jurisdiction / untested / weakly evidenced / not generated by this
   framework / not measured by this instrument. Must be distinguished from a
   justified zero after actual measurement.

5. **Possibility Compression** — Possibilities below a plausibility threshold are
   treated as functionally irrelevant. Efficient for decision-making, destructive
   during exploratory metaphysics.

6. **Reconstruction Deficit / Translation Bypass** — Evaluation may begin before the
   framework is reconstructed per its own aims, ontology, and success conditions.
   NOT yet established; must be tested against cases of careful reconstruction.

7. **Baseline Exemption** — The preferred standard is treated as neutral baseline
   rather than a framework with its own assumptions, producing asymmetric burden.

8. **Contest-to-Defeat Compression** — A raised objection is treated as stronger
   evidence of defeat than the dialectical situation warrants.
   Counter-principle: **Contested ≠ Defeated.**

## Grant's Provisional Zero Pipeline

```text
Prediction Sovereignty
→ Universalized Jurisdiction
→ Restricted Generative Admission
→ Framework translated into prediction-centered terms
→ Non-predictive successes omitted or downgraded
→ Missing demonstration treated as absence
→ Absence converted to zero
```

This is not a final diagnosis. The profile actively searches for counterexamples.

# V. GRANT VALIDATION QUESTIONS

1. What exactly does Grant believe a score of zero means?
2. Is zero "no demonstrated capacity," "actual incapacity," or "outside the model"?
3. Does he apply the same standard to his preferred framework?
4. What forms of evidence can legitimately raise a score?
5. Can non-predictive explanatory, normative, or generative success count?
6. Does he distinguish framework failure from evaluator-instrument mismatch?
7. What would falsify Prediction Sovereignty?
8. When has Grant granted a possibility despite low plausibility?
9. When has he reconstructed an opposing framework charitably?
10. Does his method change when playing offense versus defense?

# VIII. SAFEGUARDS

## Do Not

- diagnose mental illness;
- claim private motives as fact;
- convert jokes into permanent traits;
- preserve sensitive personal information unnecessarily;
- treat one bad interaction as identity;
- write profiles as prosecution briefs;
- conceal Ziggy's interpretive role;
- allow operator names to become insults;
- automatically publish private-circle profiles publicly.

## Do

- preserve uncertainty;
- attach evidence;
- search for counterexamples;
- record member objections;
- separate public and private layers;
- permit revision;
- document who made each inference;
- distinguish descriptive models from moral judgments.

# IX. PUBLIC VS PRIVATE LAYERS

The PanHandlers Grand Hall is public-facing, but core-member cognitive profiles
default to a private or restricted layer:

```text
circle/public/     — short contributor intros, public contributions, approved quotes
circle/private/    — operator analysis, debate history, unresolved tensions,
                     relationship dynamics, source-linked profile notes
```

**Nothing moves from private to public without explicit review.**

See `ETHICS_AND_CONSENT.md` for the operational policy — including the fact that
this git repository itself is public, which makes the publication gate a matter of
*what gets committed and pushed*, not just folder naming.
