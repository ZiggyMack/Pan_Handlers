## What the digs already imply

No Angles operator, motive, or trait has ever been promoted — every
dig that touched him closed at zero. Confident, not corroborated.

**The earliest thread: category confusion between meaning and matter
(AUX_00, 2026-06-15).** The very first mention of Angles anywhere in
the archive is Ziggy asking Nova to help him explain a category error
to Angles: judging systems that describe *meaning* by the standards
built for systems that describe *physical matter*. This is Z-DIRECT —
Ziggy's own position, not a claim about what Angles actually argued —
but it implies Angles was the one running that conflation in whatever
conversation prompted it, at the earliest point he appears in the
record.

**A resistance episode inside Grant's own material, with a genuine
identity question attached (SWEEP_12).** Ziggy reports "Angles"
resisting the operationalization of a stochastic-minima conversational
model Grant had proposed — *"ironically for some reason angles is so
resistant…"* — and separately calls that same temperature/minima
framework "the backbone and lifeblood" of what Angles is trying to
build, comparing it to CFA. This is the one place the archive directly
flags doubt about who "Angles" even is: the claim was filed *inside
Grant's position ledger*, with the note **"it is unclear whether
Angles is Grant, a separate participant, or a mistaken
transcription."** If Angles and Grant are the same person in some
threads, that would reframe a lot of what's below — this file doesn't
resolve it, it just makes sure the question stays visible.

**A documented vocabulary preference (SWEEP_21).** In a much later,
unrelated dig, Ziggy accommodates "Angles's preferred ontology
vocabulary" when working through a distinction between what a model
treats as existing versus what a frame merely emphasizes — suggesting
Angles has a standing preference for "ontology"-flavored framing that
Ziggy tracks and adapts to, independent of the specific topic at hand.

**Two thin, undeveloped mentions (SWEEP_03, SWEEP_05).** Angles is
also named — without any position ever being developed — as someone
with "shared awareness of the Münchhausen trilemma" (SW03) and simply
as a figure in the "opening historical background" of another dig
(SW05). Both explicitly rule any specific position `UNKNOWN`.

**The moderation-first lens.** Angles reads new conceptual proposals
through a moderation and implementation-cost lens first, before
engaging with them on their own terms. When Ziggy raised the idea
that noise or disruption might carry situational utility, Angles heard
it as an argument for defending disruptive participants' right to
stay in a conversation — and the objection started almost immediately,
before the abstract point had room to land (SWEEP_10). He's also been
reported as reluctant to discuss a framework independently of its
moderation and cost implications: raising AI implementation cost as
an objection to an idea that hadn't yet gotten past the whiteboard
stage (SWEEP_10).

The pattern that recurs across his two most substantial appearances:
Angles converts an abstract or exploratory claim into its worst-case
concrete consequence, then argues against the consequence rather than
the claim. In the noise case that consequence was "you're defending
trolls." In the AI-governance case it was religious capture. The
category-error episode (AUX_00) and the model-resistance episode
(SW12) both fit the same shape, one dig earlier each time: something
abstract gets proposed, and Angles's reported reaction moves straight
to what it would cost or threaten in practice.

**The AI-and-power concern.** Angles is reported to fear that an
AI-backed epistemic or governance system could end up validating
theism, empowering theists institutionally, or providing cover for
coercive religious authority — Ziggy's own gloss: *"I think he is
concerned about this power layer."* The sharpest version of the fear,
as reported: he refuses to risk being made to "bow down to Jesus"
through a system he didn't choose and can't contest (SWEEP_22). Nova's
later generalization of this into a worldview-neutral capture concern
(any ideology capturing the evaluative standard, not just theism) is
explicitly Nova's move, not something Angles is shown saying — but
it's a generalization that fits the shape of his original objection
well.

**What's never been demonstrated.** Nothing here has ever been shown
to be Angles' actual reasoning process rather than Ziggy's read of it.
The interpretive readings that got the closest to sticking — that
Angles reconstructs a model through its feared implementation before
engaging its intended scope (Reconstruction Bypass-adjacent), that he
extends moderation standards into domains where they don't obviously
apply (Universalized Evaluative Jurisdiction-adjacent), or that he's
simply protecting control over the conversation rather than engaging
the idea — were all explicitly quarantined in the original filing,
with the same flag repeated every time: **no direct Angles speech
exists.** There is also an unresolved lead (a remark that the AI was
"drawing with a crayon") that's never been chased down.
