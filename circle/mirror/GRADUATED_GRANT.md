## Epistemic standards

Grant runs a tight evidentiary bar and applies it consistently. He
insists that what justifies an accusation is the evidence available
*at the time it was made* — later-discovered material doesn't
retroactively rescue a judgment call (G-002). He rejects population-
level reasoning as a substitute for individualized evidence: a group
correlation doesn't license accusing a specific person (G-002). He
tests definitions at their edges rather than taking them at face
value — the son-height/spoiled-milk case wasn't a gotcha, it was a
genuine stress test of what "faith as uncertain action" actually
commits you to (G-003). When he thinks a historical attribution is
wrong (the alleged Hume "second definition" of faith), he challenges
it head-on rather than letting it stand (G-003).

## Formalism, wielded both ways

Grant reaches for formal structure when a debate gets loose, and he's
good at it. He'll bracket theology, modality, and semantic expansion
to force a fixed, checkable deduction (G-005's Formal-Scope
Isolation), and once a formalization is accepted, he holds the line:
accept the conclusion or name the premise you're rejecting — no
middle ground where you dispute the vibe without disputing a term
(G-005's Premise–Conclusion Consistency Enforcement). Notably, he
built himself an exit: he explicitly allows that a Christian
interlocutor may simply reject a premise rather than accept a forced
conclusion (G-005) — the formalism is a tool he uses on others, not a
trap he's willing to be caught in himself.

The B1/B2 opposite-compatibility demand (G-007) and the higher-reason
regress challenge (G-008) both fit this same pattern: real
philosophical instruments — the opposite-compatibility point in
particular is hard to dismiss, since a ground that's equally
compatible with an outcome and its negation genuinely hasn't explained
that outcome — deployed at moments that read less like open inquiry
and more like closing a door. The regress challenge in particular
("if you can't give me a higher reason, I win") functions as a
conversation-ending move dressed as a foundations argument: heads he
wins by declared victory, tails the game just restarts one level up.

## Cognitive flexibility, narrowly bounded

Grant will concede ground when the terms are narrowed enough — he
accepted that cognition happens outside explicit conscious reasoning
once that claim was stripped down far enough to be safe (G-004), and
he rejected the flat "conscious deduction vs. supernatural revelation"
binary as a false dichotomy (G-004). But the concessions stay
narrow and don't generalize; he polices scope aggressively once a
concession is made, and nothing about his own metaphysics, his DCT
formulation, or his God-classification has ever been put on the table
the way he demands of others.

## Trust in the AI-mediation channel is real but conditional

Grant has directly and positively distinguished Ziggy's integrated,
critical use of AI tools from "unfiltered AI output" — a genuine,
specific vote of trust, not a courtesy (G-004). But he's also willing
to name the risk directly: he's raised, unprompted, the possibility
that the assistant has been calibrated or amplified by Ziggy's own
framing over time (G-005's AI Calibration Challenge), and in a later
exchange dropped a pointed "back to debate via GPT, eh?" that reads as
more than idle teasing (G-006). Trust and suspicion of the same
channel appear to coexist without much tension for him — he uses it,
credits it when it's used well, and watches it the whole time.

## What twenty-two digs have not found

Despite being the pilot subject for every one of the circle's eight
predicted pathologies (Prediction Sovereignty, Universalized
Evaluative Jurisdiction, Generative Admission Restriction,
Unmeasured-to-Zero Conversion, Possibility Compression, Reconstruction
Bypass, Baseline Exemption, Contest-to-Defeat Compression), none has
landed. The pattern that keeps showing up instead, across sources that
have nothing else in common, is closer to *disciplined epistemic
gatekeeping with a formalist's instinct for closing doors* than
anything resembling the predicted failure modes.
