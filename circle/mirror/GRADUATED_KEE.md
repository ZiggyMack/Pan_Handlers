## The one clean line

Kee's direct contribution: *"It's Moral Obligation = Psychological
Motivation."* Everything else attributed to her is paraphrase, not
quotation — she equates moral obligation with psychological
motivation and preservation tendencies, and leans on common sense and
individuality rather than argument.

## What it triggered, and the honest caveat about it

Her claim, paired with Tom's "harm gives reasons," produced a named
operator: **Motivation/Obligation Separation** — the question "why
does suffering generate moral *obligation* rather than just
psychological *motivation*?" But the move's own stated caveat is worth
taking seriously rather than treating as boilerplate: this separation
might be **artificially imposed** on a position Tom and Kee already
hold coherently without it. Collapsing obligation into motivation
isn't obviously a mistake — it's a real position in ethics
(internalism, sentimentalism), and Kee may simply be stating it
plainly rather than failing to notice a distinction. She also anchors
one axis of a larger nine-way taxonomy separating motivation, desire-
based reasons, moral reasons, and categorical obligation from each
other — a taxonomy the record calls decisive for the whole Tom
dispute, which makes her one-line contribution more load-bearing than
its length suggests.

## The role she's actually shown playing

Beyond the one claim, Kee mostly appears as texture: common-sense
remarks and reactions that function as a signal of what reads as
intuitively compelling to a non-specialist audience, rather than as
argument in her own right. That's rated low-to-medium confidence even
in the underlying record.

## The tell

Tom, Sorta, and Tapioca all got a named position carried forward into
the packet's final disposition. **Kee did not.** Her material stayed
at the lowest tier the record uses — a bare possible omission — and
was never elevated to a labeled position the way the other three
were. That's not a knock on her point; it might just mean nobody
followed up. But it's the most honest thing this file can say about
her: the gap between what she said and what got done with it is
bigger than for anyone else on this list.
