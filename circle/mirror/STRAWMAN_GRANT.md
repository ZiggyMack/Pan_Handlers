## Moral compass, stripped of hedging

He strives to act in a way that will minimize suffering, all things
considered. That's it. That's the whole compass.

The irony is baked into the question itself: if you try to describe
it in any other way than that, he'll reject it. He stipulates his own
definition of suffering based on what he thinks humans truly mean by
"moral" — so there's no room to paraphrase him differently without him
correcting you back to this exact sentence.

## What he's actually doing when he pushes back

Protecting his definitions. His world. I don't feel tied down by any
of mine — I'll try to make my point with whatever I have available.
Grant has dug himself into a pit, and unless a ladder gets dropped
down that he thinks was assembled in a justified way, in accordance
with the specifications of his own pit, he is never crawling out.

## Regress-challenge stuff — genuine position or debate weapon?

Weapon, mostly. He often uses technicalities to avoid having to
actually answer questions, and this felt like the same move — a cheap
trick when he threw it out. That said, I think he knew it carries
real philosophical weight too, so call it a double dip on his part —
touché. But the motivation in the moment leaned much more toward
digging his heels in than toward a genuine foundations inquiry. At
least that's what it felt like at the time.

## Why he keeps engaging with me at all

He realizes the value in having an opponent on the other side of the
net — regardless of whether you like their playing style. It's the
only way to test out all the new backspin techniques you've been
learning.
