## What the digs already imply

Every claim in this section traces to a real source ID (G-002 through
G-008) — it just hasn't cleared the bar the real archive actually
requires: independent recurrence, cross-extractor confirmation, or
Grant's own answers to the validation questions he's never given.
Confident, not corroborated.

**Epistemic standards.** Grant runs a tight evidentiary bar and
applies it consistently. He insists that what justifies an accusation
is the evidence available *at the time it was made* — later-discovered
material doesn't retroactively rescue a judgment call (G-002). He
rejects population-level reasoning as a substitute for individualized
evidence: a group correlation doesn't license accusing a specific
person (G-002). He tests definitions at their edges rather than
taking them at face value — the son-height/spoiled-milk case wasn't a
gotcha, it was a genuine stress test of what "faith as uncertain
action" actually commits you to (G-003). When he thinks a historical
attribution is wrong (the alleged Hume "second definition" of faith),
he challenges it head-on rather than letting it stand (G-003).

**Formalism, wielded both ways.** Grant reaches for formal structure
when a debate gets loose, and he's good at it. He'll bracket theology,
modality, and semantic expansion to force a fixed, checkable deduction
(G-005's Formal-Scope Isolation), and once a formalization is
accepted, he holds the line: accept the conclusion or name the premise
you're rejecting — no middle ground where you dispute the vibe without
disputing a term (G-005's Premise–Conclusion Consistency Enforcement).
Notably, he built himself an exit: he explicitly allows that a
Christian interlocutor may simply reject a premise rather than accept
a forced conclusion (G-005) — the formalism is a tool he uses on
others, not a trap he's willing to be caught in himself.

The B1/B2 opposite-compatibility demand (G-007) and the higher-reason
regress challenge (G-008) both fit this same pattern: real
philosophical instruments — the opposite-compatibility point in
particular is hard to dismiss, since a ground that's equally
compatible with an outcome and its negation genuinely hasn't explained
that outcome — deployed at moments that read less like open inquiry
and more like closing a door. The regress challenge in particular
("if you can't give me a higher reason, I win") functions as a
conversation-ending move dressed as a foundations argument: heads he
wins by declared victory, tails the game just restarts one level up.

**Cognitive flexibility, narrowly bounded.** Grant will concede ground
when the terms are narrowed enough — he accepted that cognition
happens outside explicit conscious reasoning once that claim was
stripped down far enough to be safe (G-004), and he rejected the flat
"conscious deduction vs. supernatural revelation" binary as a false
dichotomy (G-004). But the concessions stay narrow and don't
generalize; he polices scope aggressively once a concession is made,
and nothing about his own metaphysics, his DCT formulation, or his
God-classification has ever been put on the table the way he demands
of others.

**Trust in the AI-mediation channel is real but conditional.** Grant
has directly and positively distinguished Ziggy's integrated, critical
use of AI tools from "unfiltered AI output" — a genuine, specific vote
of trust, not a courtesy (G-004). But he's also willing to name the
risk directly: he's raised, unprompted, the possibility that the
assistant has been calibrated or amplified by Ziggy's own framing over
time (G-005's AI Calibration Challenge), and in a later exchange
dropped a pointed "back to debate via GPT, eh?" that reads as more
than idle teasing (G-006). Trust and suspicion of the same channel
appear to coexist without much tension for him — he uses it, credits
it when it's used well, and watches it the whole time.

**What twenty-two digs have not found.** Despite being the pilot
subject for every one of the circle's eight predicted pathologies
(Prediction Sovereignty, Universalized Evaluative Jurisdiction,
Generative Admission Restriction, Unmeasured-to-Zero Conversion,
Possibility Compression, Reconstruction Bypass, Baseline Exemption,
Contest-to-Defeat Compression), none has landed. The pattern that
keeps showing up instead, across sources that have nothing else in
common, is closer to *disciplined epistemic gatekeeping with a
formalist's instinct for closing doors* than anything resembling the
predicted failure modes.

## Ziggy's direct read

No citations, no source tier, no hedging — the way I'd actually
describe him to a friend.

**Moral compass, stripped of hedging.** He strives to act in a way
that will minimize suffering, all things considered. That's it.
That's the whole compass. The irony is baked into the question
itself: if you try to describe it in any other way than that, he'll
reject it. He stipulates his own definition of suffering based on
what he thinks humans truly mean by "moral" — so there's no room to
paraphrase him differently without him correcting you back to this
exact sentence.

**What he's actually doing when he pushes back.** Protecting his
definitions. His world. I don't feel tied down by any of mine — I'll
try to make my point with whatever I have available. Grant has dug
himself into a pit, and unless a ladder gets dropped down that he
thinks was assembled in a justified way, in accordance with the
specifications of his own pit, he is never crawling out.

**Regress-challenge stuff — genuine position or debate weapon?**
Weapon, mostly. He often uses technicalities to avoid having to
actually answer questions, and this felt like the same move — a cheap
trick when he threw it out. That said, I think he knew it carries
real philosophical weight too, so call it a double dip on his part —
touché. But the motivation in the moment leaned much more toward
digging his heels in than toward a genuine foundations inquiry. At
least that's what it felt like at the time.

**Why he keeps engaging with me at all.** He realizes the value in
having an opponent on the other side of the net — regardless of
whether you like their playing style. It's the only way to test out
all the new backspin techniques you've been learning.
