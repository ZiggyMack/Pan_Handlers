## The one intervention

Sorta's full contribution to the record: *"What they define as good
actions can result in the same good outcomes from non-agential
sources. What role is agency actually playing in the account?"* —
with a second pass adding, *"Virtue and constructivist accounts
address this."*

That's it. That's the entire direct record. And it's enough to name
an operation after her: the **Outcome Value–Agent Assessment
Separation** — one of the cleanest interventions in the whole thread,
because it doesn't defend either side of the Ziggy–Tom dispute, it
just sharpens a vague worry into a precise question. She isn't arguing
consequentialism is false. She's asking what work "agency" is actually
doing in an account where a non-agential process could produce the
identical good outcome.

## Why it's sharp rather than merely correct

The move splits into three levels that were getting quietly merged
before she said anything: the value of an outcome, the rightness of
an action, and the assessment of the agent who brought it about. Her
question forces those apart. A non-agential process producing a good
outcome doesn't make agency irrelevant to morality, and it doesn't
defeat consequentialism outright — but it does mean any account that
wants agency to matter has to say *where* it matters, since it
apparently isn't doing the work at the level of outcome-value alone.

## What's still open

Whether her example shows only that agency is unnecessary for
*outcome-value* specifically, or whether it's a symptom of a bigger
gap — that the theory in question lacks any real account of action or
character at all — was never settled. That's the live question her
one sentence opened and nobody closed.
