## What the digs already imply

The "Unavailable Tale Source Package" has been the packet's central
evidence gap every time Tale's name has come up. Confident, not
corroborated.

**Where this actually starts: a friend, a book, and a shared
interest.** Tale's first appearance in the archive predates either
dispute-dig and has nothing to do with Grant at all. Ziggy, introducing
a source: *"the anatomy of genre by Truby is a recommended book my
friend Tale Recursion brought to my attention"* (DIG_AUX_05,
2026-07-17) — and the two had reportedly been discussing story as a
powerful human tool before that. Tale's own view on narrative,
genre, or anything else discussed in that conversation was never
captured; the only thing established is that he made the
recommendation and that Ziggy calls him a friend, not just a circle
member.

**A second reported role, separate from the dispute: co-builder, not
just interpreted party.** SWEEP_19 names a relationship pattern where
Ziggy and Tale reportedly try to develop broad ideas collaboratively,
with a role split forming: Ziggy and Tale as "builders," Grant as
"blocker." This is the only place in the record where Tale functions
as someone constructing ideas alongside Ziggy rather than someone
whose past wording is being fought over — filed at only medium
confidence, since neither Tale nor Grant appears directly to confirm
the framing.

**The structural role he ends up playing in the dispute itself.** Once
the Grant interpretation fight starts, Tale becomes what the record
calls an "authority-object": simultaneously the person whose meaning
is disputed and the reported authority who allegedly confirms Ziggy's
steelman over Grant's. Because he never speaks directly in either
dig, that role is described as structurally decisive but evidentially
unavailable — the whole dispute leans on what he supposedly thinks,
while what he actually thinks stays out of reach.

**The dispute Tale keeps getting pulled into.** The recurring shape,
across at least two separate packets: Grant deconstructs the literal
wording of something Tale said, derives implications from that
wording that Tale never intended, and treats the derived implication
as if it were Tale's actual position. Tale's reported response, more
than once, is a flat denial — *"No, I did not mean that."* When Ziggy
offers his own reconstruction of what Tale meant instead, Tale
reportedly recognizes it as accurate where Grant's did not land.

Ziggy's own read on the stakes, stated directly: *"this has nothing to
do with whether Tail's right or correct. It's just, is somebody
properly representing his ideas?"* — the dispute isn't really about
Tale's substantive views at all; it's a fight over interpretive
authority happening on top of Tale, between Ziggy and Grant.

**What Tale is actually shown affirming.** Once: that Ziggy is
steelmanning his position correctly and Grant is not — reported by
Ziggy as Tale's own assessment. That, and the flat denial above, are
the only two things resembling a direct Tale position anywhere in the
record. Everything else is Ziggy's description of what Tale would
presumably say, or Grant's competing description of the same.

**Grant's counter, also only reported.** Grant's side of this, relayed
the same way: that Ziggy is "mind reading Tale," and that it's
"bordering on dishonest" to represent challenging Tale on the logical
implications of his words as equivalent to claiming Tale actually
holds those implications as beliefs. That's a real distinction —
testing what a sentence commits you to is not the same claim as
asserting what someone believes — and nothing in the record settles
which side of that line the actual exchange fell on.

**The instrument this produced.** The one thing that stuck cleanly:
**self-report is authoritative but not sovereign** — Tale gets the
last word on what he meant, but not on what his wording actually
commits him to in front of others, or on what its downstream
consequences are. Ziggy's "Intended-Position Reconstruction" —
reconstruct what someone was actually defending before treating a
derived implication as their belief — is filed as a cautious,
one-instance candidate built directly out of this dispute.

**What keeps happening structurally.** The relationship-level pattern
named for this: Grant identifies a consequence, Ziggy hears it
presented as Tale's position, Grant insists he only meant the
consequence and not the belief, Ziggy shifts to arguing the
representation is functionally misleading anyway, Grant challenges
whether Ziggy has access to anyone's intent at all — and by that
point the original question about what Tale actually said has quietly
disappeared from the exchange.
