## 3. Offense/Defense Analysis

### Ziggy on offense

Ziggy’s offensive mode is characterized by accepting an opponent’s stated foundation long enough to generate unwanted implications from it. He does not primarily attack the foundation from outside. He enters it, drives it toward a limit, absorbs successive objections, and then asks what additional machinery was silently required to block the result.

The recurring offensive sequence is:

1. provisional adoption;
2. terminal stress test;
3. objection absorption;
4. implementation revision;
5. qualifier reopening;
6. shift to standard authority.

His offense is strongest when the opponent presents a simple, authoritative criterion. The simpler the criterion, the easier it is for him to expose multiple paths branching from it.

His main offensive outputs are:

* underdetermination;
* hidden safeguards;
* interpretive discretion;
* framework dependence;
* and unacknowledged jurisdiction-setting.

His offensive failure risks are:

* attacking an incomplete paraphrase;
* treating all added constraints as ad hoc;
* relying on extreme examples whose emotional force outruns their logical precision;
* and sliding from “this foundation does not uniquely yield your outcome” to “your outcome has no justification.”

### Ziggy on defense

Ziggy’s defensive mode avoids direct defense on an opponent’s chosen terrain. When a claim loses within that terrain, he narrows the concession and contests the jurisdiction.

The recurring defensive sequence is:

1. concede the internal verdict;
2. mark the framework boundary;
3. deny universal scope;
4. introduce an alternative foundation;
5. challenge the authority of the opponent’s standard.

This defense protects him from being forced to treat Grant’s epistemic standards as neutral or universal.

Its productive function is burden symmetry: Grant may rule within Grant’s framework, but must justify extending that ruling beyond it.

Its failure risk is immunization. Any criticism could be answered by changing frameworks, making genuine defeat impossible.

### Nova on offense

Nova rarely initiates aggressive substantive tests. Its offensive contribution is mostly conceptual compression and distinction-making:

* practical versus fundamental uncertainty;
* desire versus justification;
* motive versus public defensibility;
