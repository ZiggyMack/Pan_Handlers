# Decentralized Central Intelligence Agency (D.C.I.A.)

**Status:** Concept
**Track:** Intelligence / Governance
**Owner:** Ziggy + community

---

## The Problem

Current intelligence architecture is centralized in both collection AND analysis.

This creates:
- Single points of failure
- Single points of corruption
- Information bottlenecks
- Institutional blindspots
- Opacity that breeds abuse

The intelligence community was designed for a world of nation-state adversaries and hierarchical information flow. That world is gone.

## Architecture

### Layer 1: Distributed Collection

**What this means:**
- Open-source intelligence (OSINT) at scale
- Citizen journalism integration
- Academic research synthesis
- Automated monitoring with transparent criteria

**Not:**
- Spying on citizens
- Secret surveillance
- Covert operations

We collect what's **already public** but synthesize it intelligently.

### Layer 2: Multi-Source Validation

**Cross-validate using:**
- Multiple AI architectures (Nova, Gemini, Grok, Claude)
- Multiple human analysts
- Multiple methodological approaches

No single source. No single point of manipulation.

### Layer 3: Centralized Synthesis

**Unified analysis that:**
- Aggregates validated information
- Applies consistent analytical frameworks
- Produces confidence-weighted assessments
- Documents methodology transparently

### Layer 4: Public Intelligence Products

**What citizens get:**
- Intelligence assessments on matters of public interest
- Confidence levels and uncertainty
- Methodology documentation
- Ability to verify and challenge

## Classification Model

### What's Public by Default:
- Analytical methodology
- Assessment conclusions (with confidence levels)
- Source types (not specific sources where protection needed)
- Historical analyses

### What's Time-Limited Classification:
- Specific source identities
- Ongoing operations details
- Information that could enable harm

### Classification Principles:
- Default to open
- Classification requires justification
- Time limits on all classification
- Independent review of classification decisions

## Why This Matters

Intelligence shouldn't be a black box that citizens have to trust.

It should be infrastructure that citizens can verify.

**D.C.I.A. proves that:**
- Distributed collection is more robust than centralized surveillance
- Open methodology produces better analysis than secret methods
- Public accountability improves, not hinders, intelligence work
- Citizens can be partners in national understanding, not subjects of it

## The Relationship to ABI

**ABI** = Investigative intelligence (like FBI) made transparent
**D.C.I.A.** = Strategic intelligence (like CIA) made distributed and open

Same principles:
- Transparency
- Accountability
- Open methods
- Public service

Different functions:
- ABI: Investigations of specific matters
- D.C.I.A.: Strategic understanding of the world

Together, they demonstrate that the entire intelligence function can work better in the open.
