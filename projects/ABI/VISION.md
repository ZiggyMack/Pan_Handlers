## The Problem

Intelligence agencies operate in opacity, breeding mistrust and enabling abuse.

The information architecture is Stone Age:
- Disconnected databases
- Siloed knowledge
- Institutional amnesia
- No public accountability
- Methods hidden behind classification

We've seen what happens when intelligence agencies operate without transparency:
- COINTELPRO
- Mass surveillance
- Politicized investigations
- Broken public trust

## Architecture Principles

### What Gets Public:
- Investigation methodology
- Evidence provenance chains
- Entity relationship structures (redacted for privacy)
- Decision audit logs
- Confidence assessments

### What Gets Redacted:
- Personal identifying information (unless public figures in public capacity)
- Ongoing investigation details that could compromise outcomes
- Sources and methods that would endanger people
- Information that could enable harm

### The Accountability Model:
- All redactions logged with justification
- Time-limited classification (auto-declassify after period)
- Independent review of redaction decisions
- Public can challenge redactions through process
