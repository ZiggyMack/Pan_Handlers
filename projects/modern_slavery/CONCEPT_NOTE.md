**Status:** Concept

**Owner:** Daniel (lead) + Nova + Ziggy

## The Problem

The US prison system is structurally optimized for warehousing and recidivism, not transformation or reintegration.

The brutal truth:
- 2.3 million people incarcerated (highest rate in the world)
- 76% recidivism rate within 5 years
- System designed to punish, not rehabilitate
- "Corrections" that correct nothing
- Millions of lives wasted in a system that serves no one

Not victims. Not offenders. Not society.

The 13th Amendment abolished slavery "except as punishment for crime." We built a new slavery and called it justice.

---

## How Nyquist Helps

Apply the same frameworks we use for identity and transformation to the hardest problem:

1. **Identity Manifolds for Personal Transformation**
   - Track how incarcerated individuals change over time
   - Measure actual rehabilitation, not just compliance
   - Identify what interventions actually work

2. **Temporal Stability Metrics**
   - Monitor rehabilitation progress across incarceration
   - Detect when people are ready for reintegration
   - Track post-release stability

3. **Omega Synthesis Sessions**
   - Bridge Daniel's lived experience with policy design
   - Synthesize across perspectives (formerly incarcerated, guards, families, victims)
   - Design interventions that honor all stakeholders

4. **Cross-Validation Frameworks**
   - Compare different intervention approaches
   - Identify what actually reduces recidivism
   - Evidence-based policy, not political theater

## The Redesign Vision

### What a Transformed System Looks Like:

**Instead of warehousing:**
- Education and skill development
- Mental health treatment
- Addiction recovery
- Family connection maintenance
- Reintegration preparation from day one

**Instead of punishment for punishment's sake:**
- Accountability that heals
- Restorative justice options
- Victim-offender mediation where appropriate
- Community restoration

**Instead of recidivism machines:**
- Measured transformation outcomes
- Support systems that continue post-release
- Employment pipelines
- Housing stability programs
